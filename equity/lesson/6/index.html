Foundation · before company

Why industry precedes company.

Best company trong industry tệ thường thua average company trong industry tốt. Industry structure quyết định profit pool — cap trên long-term ROIC. Trước khi pick stock, ranking industry attractiveness là step quan trọng.

α
"Bet on the jockey, ride a fast horse"
Buffett: "khi management tốt gặp business tệ, business reputation thường thắng". Industry quality thường constrains company outcome.
β
Profit pool varies 10× by industry
Software industry ROIC trung bình 25-30%; airline industry trung bình 5-8%. Same execution effort = vastly different return potential.
γ
Macro → industry → company workflow
Top-down: macro outlook (rate, GDP, FX) → identify hot/cold sectors → pick best company within each.
δ
Sector rotation = industry-driven alpha
Pro PMs rotate sector based on macro view (cyclical sectors khi expansion; defensive khi recession). Industry analysis = primary tool.

Top-down workflow

Macro → industry → company.

  1. 1 · Macro outlook GDP cycle (expansion/peak/recession/recovery), monetary policy (rate trajectory), fiscal stance, FX, commodity prices, geopolitical risk. Output: 12-24 month thesis cho economic regime.
  2. 2 · Industry attractiveness ranking Filter industries phù hợp regime: rising rate → financials thường benefit, REIT & utilities thường suffer. Recession → defensive (consumer staples, healthcare). Recovery → cyclical (industrials, materials).
  3. 3 · Industry structure analysis Trong industry chọn: Porter forces, life cycle stage, structure type. Identify leaders, laggards, disruption risk.
  4. 4 · Company selection Trong industry attractive: pick company với strongest competitive position (m05 past/present + m07 forecasting analysis).

§ Bottom-up alternative — start với company picks regardless industry. Stock pickers like Lynch, Buffett. Both valid; CFA L1 chủ yếu test top-down framework.

Classification systems

Three classification systems.

CFA L1 mention 3 cách classify industries: commercial (GICS, ICB, RGS), government (NAICS, ISIC, SIC), statistical (cluster theo correlation). Plus 1 traditional CFA category: cyclical / defensive / growth.

Commercial systems
GICS
Global Industry Classification Standard (S&P + MSCI). 11 sectors, 25 industry groups, 74 industries, 163 sub-industries. Phổ biến nhất cho equity research và index construction.
ICB
Industry Classification Benchmark (FTSE Russell). 11 industries, 20 supersectors. Tương tự GICS.
RGS
Russell Global Sectors. 9 sectors. Used by Russell indices.
Government + Statistical
NAICS / SIC / ISIC
NAICS = North American Industry Classification (US/Canada/Mexico). SIC = older Standard Industrial. ISIC = UN system. Government statistical purposes (employment, output stats).
Statistical clustering
Group companies theo similarity in historical return correlations hoặc business metric clustering. Useful cho risk modeling, không phải fundamental analysis.
Traditional CFA
Cyclical · Defensive · Growth — not formal taxonomy nhưng practical sector view used in equity research.

Sensitivity to economic cycle

Cyclical vs Defensive.

Cyclical industries
Definition
Earnings highly sensitive to economic cycle. Big swings up trong expansion, down trong recession.
Examples
Auto, airline, hotel, luxury goods, construction, industrial machinery, semiconductor, banking, asset management.
Beta
High β (> 1.0) — outperform broad market khi expansion, underperform khi recession.
Sub-types
Consumer cyclical (auto, leisure, retail) vs Industrial cyclical (machinery, materials).
Defensive (non-cyclical)
Definition
Earnings stable across cycle. Demand essential, ít elastic theo income.
Examples
Consumer staples (food, beverage, household goods), utilities (electricity, water), healthcare (pharma, hospitals), telecom.
Beta
Low β (< 1.0) — relatively stable, outperform khi recession (relatively).
Trade-off
Stable nhưng growth thấp. Investor accept lower upside để có lower downside.

§ Growth industries là category 3 (separate): industries growing faster than economy regardless cycle (vd cloud computing, EV battery, gen AI). Có thể overlap cyclical hoặc defensive — tech growth thường cyclical, healthcare growth thường defensive.

Porter's framework

Porter's 5 Forces.

Michael Porter (Harvard, 1979) framework xác định industry profitability potential. 5 forces compete cho profit pool: high force = lower industry profitability (profit drained away); low force = higher industry profitability (profit retained).

§ The 5 Forces (memorize)
1 Threat of new entrants
2 Bargaining power of suppliers
3 Bargaining power of buyers
4 Threat of substitutes
5 Industry rivalry (existing competitors)
High force = bad for industry profitability. Vd suppliers high power → suppliers extract margin (Microsoft mua chip Intel; Intel có pricing power). Industry attractive khi tất cả 5 forces low.

§ Pneumonic — "NEW SUB" / "BUYER" / "RIVAL": New entrants, sUpplier, sUBstitute, BUYER, RIVALry. Hoặc 3 categories: vertical (suppliers + buyers), horizontal (entrants + substitutes + rivalry).

07.06 — Industry 08 / 30
Force 1 · new entrants

Threat of new entrants + barriers.

High threat = new players có thể join → existing players phải lower price hoặc invest defensive → lower industry margin. Low threat (high barriers) = incumbents protected.

  1. Capital requirement Capital-intensive industries (telecom, semiconductor fab, airline) = high barrier; phải invest billions trước khi compete. Software, consulting = low capital, easy entry.
  2. Economies of scale Industries với significant scale advantages (Walmart, Amazon distribution, semiconductor fab) — new entrant phải đạt scale tương đương để cost-competitive → khó.
  3. Brand & switching cost Strong brand (Coca-Cola, Apple) → customer loyal khó switch. High switching cost (enterprise software như SAP, banking core systems) → customer captive.
  4. Regulation / licensing Pharma (FDA approval), banking (regulator license), telecom (spectrum auction), utilities (rate regulation). Regulator quyết định ai vào.
  5. Network effects Platforms với network effects (Facebook, LinkedIn, Visa) — value proposition tăng theo số users. New entrant cần critical mass impossible.

§ Barriers to exit cũng important — nếu cao (long-term contract, specialized assets, employee severance) thì capacity stuck even khi profitability collapse → continued price war (vd US airline pre-consolidation).

07.06 — Industry 09 / 30
Forces 2 + 3 · vertical bargaining

Suppliers + Buyers bargaining power.

Force 2 — Supplier power
High when
Few suppliers (concentrated). No substitute input. Switching cost high. Supplier can forward-integrate. Industry purchased small % of supplier output.
Examples
Intel/AMD đối với PC OEMs (concentrated supplier). Boeing engine vendors (GE, Rolls-Royce). Chip foundries (TSMC).
Effect
Suppliers extract margin. Industry profit thấp dù demand strong.
Force 3 — Buyer power
High when
Few buyers (concentrated). Buyer purchase large % of industry output. Standardized product (low switching cost). Buyer can backward-integrate. Buyer price-sensitive.
Examples
Walmart vs suppliers (massive buyer). Auto OEM vs parts suppliers. Hospital group vs medical device firms.
Effect
Buyers force price down. Industry margin compressed.

§ Both vertical forces test concentration → concentrated side wins. Industry attractive khi both suppliers + buyers fragmented (high # players, low individual leverage). Vd luxury goods firms — many small suppliers + many individual buyers → high industry margin.

07.06 — Industry 10 / 30
Forces 4 + 5 · alternatives + competition

Substitutes + Rivalry.

Force 4 — Substitutes
Definition
Different product/service that satisfies same customer need. Khác từ direct competitor (cùng product); substitute = alternative way để fulfill need.
Examples
Coffee ↔ Tea, Energy drink. Airline ↔ High-speed rail (Europe, China). DVD ↔ Streaming (Netflix killed Blockbuster). Taxi ↔ Ride-hail ↔ Public transit.
High when
Substitute price-performance attractive. Switching cost low. Substitute available + visible.
Effect
Cap pricing power. Disruption risk (industry can be wiped — Kodak vs digital).
Force 5 — Industry Rivalry
High when
Many competitors of similar size. Slow industry growth (zero-sum competition). High fixed cost (must utilize capacity → price war). Low product differentiation. High exit barriers (stuck in price war).
Examples
US airlines pre-consolidation. Memory chip (DRAM). Vietnamese banking sector. Restaurant industry generally.
Low when
Few large players (oligopoly). Differentiated products. Growing industry. Implicit coordination on pricing.

07.06 — Industry 11 / 30
Industry life cycle

Five stages.

  1. 1 · Embryonic — early New industry. Slow growth (chưa adoption). High product price. High failure rate. Few players, R&D-heavy. Vd quantum computing 2026, gene therapy 2010s, blockchain 2015.
  2. 2 · Growth — accelerating Adoption accelerate. Rapid revenue growth. Margins improve (scale economies). Many entrants attracted. Vd Internet 1995-2000, smartphone 2008-2015, EV 2020-now.
  3. 3 · Shakeout — consolidation Growth slowing. Excess capacity. Marginal players exit/acquired. Margin pressure. Survivors emerge stronger. Vd US auto 1920s, dot-com 2000-2003, EV expected ~2027-2030.
  4. 4 · Mature — saturation Growth ≈ GDP. Industry consolidated (oligopoly common). High barriers to entry. Stable margins (pricing discipline). Cash-generative. Vd US banking, beverage, telecom, autos.
  5. 5 · Decline — shrinking Negative growth. Demand replaced by substitute or obsolete. Players exit or pivot. Vd print newspapers, landline phones, DVD rental, traditional cable TV.

07.06 — Industry 12 / 30
Microeconomic structures

Four structures.

§ Competition spectrum (perfect → monopoly)
Perfect competition: many sellers · identical product · price-taker · zero economic profit
Monopolistic competition: many sellers · differentiated product · some pricing power · low long-term profit
Oligopoly: few sellers · differentiated or homogeneous · interdependent pricing · sustained profit possible
Monopoly: single seller · unique product · price-maker · high sustained profit
Move từ left → right: fewer players, more differentiation, higher pricing power, higher long-term profit. Industry attractive khi gần monopoly end. Most real industries = monopolistic comp hoặc oligopoly.
§ Examples by structure Perfect competition: agricultural commodities (wheat, corn) — closest real-world.
Monopolistic comp: restaurant industry, retail apparel, consumer electronics.
Oligopoly: US airlines (post-consolidation), beverage (Coke/Pepsi), telecom carriers, auto.
Monopoly: utility companies (regulated), local cable provider (historically), pharma during patent period.

Strategic frameworks

SWOT + PESTLE.

SWOT — internal + external
Strengths (internal +)
Brand strength, IP/patents, scale, talent, distribution network. Within company control.
Weaknesses (internal −)
Capital structure (over-levered), legacy tech, lacking talent, customer concentration. Within control to fix.
Opportunities (external +)
New geographies, adjacent products, regulatory tailwind, competitor weakness. Outside control but actionable.
Threats (external −)
Substitutes, new entrants, regulation tightening, FX, geopolitical. Outside control, must adapt.
PESTLE — macro environment
Political
Government stability, trade policy, tax, sanctions. Vd US-China trade war affect tech supply chain.
Economic
GDP, rate, FX, inflation, unemployment. Affect demand & cost.
Social
Demographics (aging EU, young SEA), consumer preferences (ESG, healthy food), culture shifts.
Technological
Disruption (AI, EV, blockchain), automation, R&D capability.
Legal
Antitrust, consumer protection, employment law, IP enforcement.
Environmental
Climate, ESG mandate, carbon pricing, water scarcity, biodiversity.

§ SWOT focus on company-level internal/external; PESTLE focus on industry/macro external. Use SWOT cho stock pick; PESTLE cho sector outlook.

07.06 — Industry 14 / 30
Profit pool decomposition

Value chain + strategic groups.

  1. Value chain analysis Trace profit pool from raw material → component → assembly → distribution → retail → end customer. Identify which stage capture margin. Vd smartphone industry: chip (high margin), assembly (low margin — Foxconn 1-2%), brand (high margin — Apple). Investor focus high-margin stages.
  2. Strategic groups Companies trong same industry pursue similar strategy = strategic group. Vd luxury hotel (Four Seasons, Ritz-Carlton) vs budget (Holiday Inn Express, Motel 6). Different group = different competitive dynamics dù cùng industry. Compare within group cho more relevant peer benchmark.
  3. 3 generic competitive strategies (Porter) Cost leadership: lowest cost producer (Walmart, Ryanair). Differentiation: unique value premium pricing (Apple, Tesla, LVMH). Focus: serve niche segment (Ferrari super-luxury, Vans skater niche).

§ Stuck in middle = no clear strategy → typically poor performer. Pick a lane: cost OR differentiate OR niche.

07.06 — Industry 15 / 30
Synthesis

Industry attractiveness scorecard.

§ Attractive industry checklist (1) Low threat new entrants — high barriers (capital, scale, brand, regulation, network).
(2) Low supplier power — fragmented suppliers, alternative inputs.
(3) Low buyer power — fragmented customers, differentiated product, switching cost.
(4) Low substitute threat — no good alternative, high switching cost.
(5) Low rivalry — few large players, growing market, differentiated, low fixed cost.
(6) Life cycle stage = growth or mature (avoid decline).
(7) Structure = oligopoly or monopolistic competition with strong brand. Avoid pure perfect competition.

§ Score industry 1-5 trên mỗi pillar. Total 35 = perfectly attractive (rare). Most real industries 18-25. Pick top quartile cho stock universe.

Practice · Question 01 · Original

Identify the force.

§ Vignette
Vietnamese smartphone assemblers depend on Qualcomm and MediaTek for nearly all of their application processor chips. The two chip vendors face limited competition, hold patent portfolios that prevent direct substitution, and frequently increase prices during industry up-cycles. Vietnamese assemblers cannot easily backward-integrate due to fab capital intensity. Which of Porter's 5 Forces is most elevated for the Vietnamese assembler industry?
  1. AThreat of new entrants — capital and patents make it easy for new chip vendors to disrupt.
  2. BBargaining power of suppliers — concentrated chip vendors with patent protection extract margin from assemblers, who cannot easily switch or backward-integrate.
  3. CIndustry rivalry — many smartphone assemblers compete fiercely for chip allocation.

Hint · Question 01

Spot the leverage.

§ Keywords Qualcomm + MediaTek + concentrated + patents + can't backward-integrate. Ai có leverage trong relationship này?
  1. Hint 1 Test "supplier power high" gồm: (a) few suppliers, (b) no substitute input, (c) switching cost high, (d) supplier can forward-integrate, (e) buyer can't backward-integrate. Tất cả ✓.
  2. Hint 2 Vietnamese assembler = buyer industry. Qualcomm/MediaTek = suppliers. Suppliers concentrated + patent protected = supplier power high → drain margin từ assembler.
  3. Hint 3 Đáp án A đảo ngược logic — patents make entry khó, không "easy to disrupt". Đáp án C confuse vertical (suppliers) với horizontal (rivalry between assemblers).

Dịch đề.

§ Vignette
Các nhà lắp ráp smartphone Việt Nam phụ thuộc gần như toàn bộ vào Qualcomm và MediaTek cho application processor chips. Hai chip vendors face limited competition, sở hữu patent portfolio ngăn substitute trực tiếp, và thường tăng price trong industry up-cycle. Vietnamese assemblers không thể dễ dàng backward-integrate vì fab capital intensity quá cao. Trong Porter's 5 Forces, force nào elevated nhất cho industry lắp ráp Việt Nam?
  1. AThreat of new entrants — capital và patents khiến new chip vendors dễ disrupt.
  2. BBargaining power of suppliers — concentrated chip vendors với patent protection extract margin từ assemblers, vốn không thể switch hay backward-integrate.

07.06 — Industry 21 / 30
Answer · Question 01

Answer: B — supplier power.

✓ Correct — B
Supplier power test gồm 5 conditions, all ✓: (1) few suppliers (chỉ Qualcomm + MediaTek concentrated); (2) no substitute input (patent protection ngăn); (3) switching cost high (re-design board cho different chip); (4) supplier có pricing power "frequently increase prices"; (5) buyer không thể backward-integrate (fab capital intensity = barrier). Result: chip vendors extract margin → Vietnamese assembler industry profit pool nhỏ. Apple, Samsung là exception vì họ design own silicon → reduce supplier dependency. Vietnamese assemblers không có scale.
✗ Wrong — A
Đảo ngược logic — capital intensity và patent protection tăng entry barriers, làm threat of new entrants thấp hơn, không phải dễ disrupt. Đáp án A misread Porter framework.
✗ Wrong — C
Sai phần phân loại force — assembler-to-assembler competition là industry rivalry (Force 5), force horizontal. Câu hỏi mô tả vertical relationship (assembler vs chip supplier) → Force 2 supplier power. Đáp án C confuse 2 forces.
Key takeaway
Supplier power high khi: (1) concentrated, (2) no substitute, (3) high switching cost, (4) supplier can forward-integrate, (5) buyer can't backward-integrate. Vertical force (suppliers/buyers) khác horizontal (rivalry/entrants/substitutes). Identify which side of value chain question is testing trước khi pick force.

Life cycle stage.

§ Vignette
The global EV industry has the following characteristics in 2026: revenue growth has decelerated from 60% per year (2018-2022) to ~25% per year, several smaller manufacturers have filed bankruptcy, leading players (Tesla, BYD) have begun acquiring weaker competitors, gross margins are under pressure due to price competition, and excess production capacity exists in China. Which life cycle stage best describes the global EV industry?
  1. AGrowth stage — revenue is still growing 25% per year, faster than GDP, indicating the industry remains in expansion.
  2. BShakeout stage — decelerating growth, bankruptcies of marginal players, M&A consolidation, margin pressure, and excess capacity are textbook shakeout features.
  3. CMature stage — the presence of dominant leaders (Tesla, BYD) and consolidation indicates industry has reached maturity.

Spot the stage signature.

§ Keywords Growth decelerate + bankruptcies + M&A consolidation + margin pressure + excess capacity. 5 signature của một stage cụ thể.

Answer · Question 02

Answer: B — shakeout.

✓ Correct — B
Shakeout stage = transition giữa growth → mature, có 5 signature đặc trưng all ✓: (1) growth decelerating (60% → 25%); (2) bankruptcies of marginal players; (3) M&A consolidation (Tesla/BYD acquire weaker); (4) margin pressure (price competition); (5) excess capacity. Đây là pattern classic shakeout (US auto 1920s, dot-com 2000-2003, EV 2024-2027). Investment implication: surviving leaders emerge stronger với pricing power; weaker players bị weeded out.
✗ Wrong — A
Sai vì growth stage = accelerating growth + improving margins + many new entrants. Fact pattern có decelerating growth + margin pressure + bankruptcies — opposite of growth signatures. 25%/năm vẫn cao but trajectory matters.
✗ Wrong — C
Sai vì mature stage = growth ≈ GDP (~3-5%), consolidation đã xong với stable oligopoly. EV vẫn growing 25% (much above GDP) và consolidation đang diễn ra chưa kết thúc → chưa mature. Mature stage đến sau shakeout.
Key takeaway
5 stages signature: Embryonic (slow growth, high failure, R&D heavy); Growth (accelerate growth, many entrants, margin improving); Shakeout (decelerate growth, bankruptcies, M&A, margin pressure, excess capacity); Mature (growth ≈ GDP, oligopoly, stable margin); Decline (negative growth, exit, substitute won). Practice · Question 03 · Original

SWOT placement.

§ Vignette
An analyst is preparing a SWOT analysis for a Vietnamese pharmaceutical manufacturer. Where in the SWOT framework should each of the following be placed?
(i) The company's vertically integrated API production facility (the only one of its scale in Vietnam).
(ii) Pending Ministry of Health regulation requiring stricter bioequivalence testing for generic drugs.
(iii) Aging demographics in Vietnam expected to drive 8% annual growth in chronic-disease drug demand.
(iv) The company's high financial leverage (Debt/EBITDA = 5.2× vs industry median of 2.0×).
  1. A(i) Strength · (ii) Threat · (iii) Opportunity · (iv) Weakness — internal factors: (i) and (iv); external factors: (ii) and (iii).
  2. B(i) Strength · (ii) Weakness · (iii) Strength · (iv) Threat — all are competitive considerations affecting the company's market position.
  3. C(i) Opportunity · (ii) Strength · (iii) Threat · (iv) Weakness — internal/external classification depends on direct controllability rather than origin.

Spot internal vs external.

§ Keywords SWOT 2 axes: internal vs external · positive vs negative. Internal positive = S; internal negative = W. External positive = O; external negative = T.

Answer · Question 03

Answer: A.

✓ Correct — A
SWOT 2 trục: internal vs external + positive vs negative. (i) Strength: vertically integrated API facility = company asset (internal) + unique scale advantage (positive). (ii) Threat: Ministry regulation = outside company control (external) + stricter testing burden (negative). Sai logic — internal/external không phụ thuộc "controllability" mà phụ thuộc origin. API facility là internal (company-owned) dù không control hoàn toàn supply chain. Demographics là external dù affect company. Classification rule based on where it originates, not what management can change.
Key takeaway
SWOT 2×2 matrix: Internal × Positive = Strength; Internal × Negative = Weakness; External × Positive = Opportunity; External × Negative = Threat. Internal = company-owned assets/decisions/balance sheet. External = macro/regulatory/competitor/market. Decide internal/external first based on origin, then positive/negative.
