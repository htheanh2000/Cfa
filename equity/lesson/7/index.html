Bridge · past → value

Why forecast?

Valuation models (DCF, residual income, dividend discount) đều đòi hỏi future cash flow. Past performance là input, không phải output. Forecast = process biến historical analysis (m05) + industry outlook (m06) thành quantified future expected — input cho m08 valuation.

α
All valuation = forecast under uncertainty
Stock value = PV of future cash flows. Phải project. Differences in forecast assumptions explain phần lớn variance trong analyst price targets.
β
Forecast = anchor + judgment
Anchor = historical pattern + peer benchmark + industry growth. Judgment = adjust cho expected change (M&A, regulation, cycle, mgmt strategy). Không pure extrapolation; không pure speculation.
γ
Multiple scenarios — không single number
Single point forecast = misleading. Build base + bull + bear scenarios. Probability-weight để get expected value + understanding of dispersion.
δ
Test reasonability — sanity check
If forecast implies revenue gấp 5× trong 3 năm — likely unrealistic. Check vs industry growth, market share cap, capacity constraint.

Time structure of forecast

Forecast horizon.

Explicit period
Length
Thường 5-10 năm. Đủ dài để growth normalize về sustainable rate; đủ ngắn để forecast còn meaningful.
Detail
Year-by-year forecast revenue, costs, capex, working capital, financing. Drivers articulated explicitly.
Cycle considerations
Cyclical company: explicit period nên cover ≥ 1 full cycle (peak-to-peak). Otherwise terminal anchored to peak hoặc trough → biased.
Terminal value
Concept
PV of cash flows beyond explicit period, captured trong single terminal value. Thường 50-70% of total enterprise value cho mature company.
Methods
(a) Perpetuity growth = FCFn+1 / (WACC − g). (b) Exit multiple = EBITDAn × multiple. (c) Convergence = ROIC converges to WACC over time.
Sustainable g
Long-term g phải ≤ long-term GDP growth (otherwise company becomes infinite share of economy). Practical: 2-3% p.a. cho mature firm.

§ Terminal value sensitivity = #1 source of valuation error. Small changes trong perpetuity g gây huge changes trong PV. Always sensitivity-test terminal assumptions.

Two forecast directions

Top-down vs bottom-up.

Top-down
Approach
Macro GDP → industry size → market share → company revenue. Drill down từ aggregate.
Use case
Cyclical industries, broad-market consumer goods, financials. Where macro is dominant driver.
Strength
Capture macro-cycle dynamics. Consistent với economic forecast.
Weakness
Can miss company-specific drivers (new product launch, market share shift).
Bottom-up
Approach
Forecast từng segment / product / customer / geography → sum up to company. Build từ unit economics.
Use case
Tech, pharma, niche industrials. Where company-specific drivers dominant (new drug pipeline, product launch).
Strength
Captures company-specific story. Mgmt guidance nhiều khi bottom-up.
Weakness
Sum-of-parts có thể overstate hoặc miss macro headwind. Less consistent với industry view.

§ Best practice — dùng cả 2 approaches independently và reconcile. Discrepancy reveal hidden assumption hoặc opportunity (vd top-down says industry contracts but bottom-up sees market share gain → company outperform).

Revenue · 3 methods

Revenue forecast — 3 methods.

  1. Method 1 — Volume × Price decomposition Revenue = Volume × ASP. Forecast separately: Volume (units sold, customers, transactions) → driven by demand growth + market share. Price (ASP, mix shift, regional premium) → driven by inflation + pricing power. Best cho discrete-product company (auto, smartphone, F&B).
  2. Method 2 — Market share approach (top-down) Industry size × Company market share = Revenue. Forecast industry size (TAM growth) + company share evolution. Best khi industry data available (Euromonitor, Nielsen, IBISWorld).
  3. Method 3 — Segment / customer build-up (bottom-up) Sum revenue by segment / business line / geography / top customer. Useful cho diversified company hoặc B2B với concentrated customer base. Vd Microsoft: Productivity + Cloud + Personal Computing segments forecast riêng.
§ Reconciliation Apply ≥ 2 methods và reconcile. Discrepancy = signal need to revisit assumption. Vd bottom-up build-up gives 15% revenue growth; top-down market-share gives 8% — gap raises question: market share gain assumption realistic?

Cost · variable vs fixed

Cost forecast.

§ Variable vs Fixed cost decomposition
Total Cost = Variable Cost per unit × Volume + Fixed Cost
Variable: scales với volume (raw material, packaging, sales commission, shipping). Forecast = unit cost trend × forecast volume. Fixed: capacity-based (rent, salary base, depreciation, interest). Forecast = current level × inflation × step-changes (capex bring new capacity).
Cost forecast methods
% of revenue
Simple: forecast cost = historical % × forecast revenue. Works cho stable margin company. Hidden assumption: cost behavior linear với revenue.
Trend extrapolation
Take historical cost trajectory + extrapolate. Works for fixed cost (G&A growing với inflation).
Bottom-up build
Forecast each cost component separately: COGS by input, SG&A by department, R&D by project. Most rigorous nhưng heavy lift.
Operating leverage
Concept
High fixed cost = high operating leverage. Small revenue change → big margin change (both ways). Airline, hotel, factory = high leverage.
Forecast implication
Doubling revenue cho high-leverage firm → margin expand significantly. Halving revenue → margin collapse. Don't extrapolate margin linear với revenue cho high-leverage company.
Vietnam example
Vietnam Airlines: ~70% fixed cost (aircraft lease + crew + airport). 10% revenue swing → ~30% EBIT swing.

Margins · capex · working capital

Three balance sheet drivers.

  1. Margin trajectory Forecast margin path: improve (scale economies, mix shift, pricing power), flat (mature), decline (competition, input cost, regulation). Anchor vs peer median; don't assume permanent margin expansion above industry leader.
  2. Capex — maintenance vs growth Maintenance capex ≈ depreciation (replace existing capacity). Growth capex = additional investment expand capacity. Forecast separately. Capex ratio (capex/revenue) hoặc capex intensity (capex/sales) anchored vs historical + industry. Track ROIC on incremental capex.
  3. Working capital Forecast DSO, DIO, DPO based on past trend + industry benchmark. Compute working capital change = ΔAR + ΔInventory − ΔAP. Affects cash flow but not earnings. Growing company often has working capital build (cash drain).
  4. Tax + financing Effective tax rate: anchor vs statutory + historical. Country mix matters (geographic shift can change effective rate). Financing assumptions: debt level, interest rate trajectory, dividend payout, buyback pace.

Pro-forma model build

3-statement integrated model.

Pro-forma = forecast 3 financial statements (IS, BS, CF) integrated sao cho cash flow statement reconcile income statement và balance sheet changes. Disciplined model exposes inconsistencies trong forecast assumptions.

  1. 1 · Income Statement (top-down) Revenue → COGS → Gross Profit → SG&A + R&D → EBIT → Interest → Pre-tax → Tax → Net Income → EPS. Build từ revenue forecast, apply margin assumptions từng line.
  2. 2 · Balance Sheet (driven by IS + assumptions) Assets: PP&E (last + capex − dep), AR (DSO × revenue/365), Inventory (DIO × COGS/365), Cash (plug). Liabilities: AP (DPO × COGS/365), Debt (per financing plan). Equity: opening + Net Income − Dividend.
  3. 3 · Cash Flow Statement (reconcile IS + BS) CFO = NI + Dep − ΔWC. CFI = −Capex + Disposals. CFF = New Debt − Repayment − Dividends + Equity Issuance. Net Cash Change = sum. Plug to BS Cash. Validation: BS must balance every year.
  4. 4 · Iterate & sanity check Check: ROIC trajectory makes sense. Margin doesn't permanently exceed industry leader. Cash position doesn't go negative. Debt covenant respected. Tax rate consistent.

Scenario analysis

3-scenario framework.

Single point forecast hides uncertainty. 3-scenario framework — Base / Bull / Bear — capture range of plausible outcomes. Each scenario has internally-consistent assumption set + probability weight.

Base case (~50%)
Assumption
Most likely path. Industry growth at consensus. Company executes as guided. Margin in middle of historical band. No major regulation/disruption.
Output
Probability-weighted center of valuation distribution. "Fair value" estimate.
Bull case (~25%)
Assumption
Favorable scenarios: market share gain, pricing power realized, new product hit, favorable regulation, M&A accretive. Margin top of band.
Output
Upside fair value. Test "what could go right".
Bear case (~25%)
Assumption
Adverse scenarios: market share loss, competition intensifies, key product fail, regulation tightens, recession. Margin bottom of band.
Output
Downside fair value. Stress test resilience. Identify breaking points.

§ Probability-weighted: Expected value = 0.5 × Base + 0.25 × Bull + 0.25 × Bear. Decision rule: buy if current price < expected value − margin of safety; avoid if current price > expected value.

Sensitivity analysis

Sensitivity + tornado chart.

  1. One-variable sensitivity Hold all assumptions constant. Vary 1 input across range. Observe valuation output change. Reveals which assumption matter most.
  2. Two-variable sensitivity (data table) Vary 2 inputs simultaneously. Vd terminal growth (rows) × WACC (columns) cho DCF model. Shows valuation matrix; useful cho key driver pair.
  3. Tornado chart Rank inputs by valuation impact. Bar chart: longest bar at top = most-sensitive input. Identifies where to focus diligence: spend time refining input #1, not input #10.
  4. Monte Carlo (advanced) Random sample inputs từ distribution (normal/triangular/etc), run thousands simulations, output distribution of valuation. CFA L1 mention nhưng không calc; CFA L2/L3 deep.
§ Common most-sensitive inputs DCF: terminal growth (g), WACC, year-5 margin. Multiples: peer multiple chosen, EBITDA. Residual income: ROE persistence, terminal book value.

Pitfalls

Common forecasting errors.

E1
Hockey stick
Forecast assumes sharp acceleration in years 3-5 without operational basis. Pattern phổ biến cho startups; analysts thường too optimistic about year 3+. Sanity check: industry growth, market share cap.
E2
Permanent margin expansion above leader
Assume company eventually exceed industry leader's margin. Market structure prevents this absent unique advantage. Cap margin assumption at peer best-in-class.
E3
Anchoring on management guidance
Mgmt has incentive to be optimistic (compensation tied to forecast). Past guidance accuracy matters: company chronically miss → discount; chronically beat → conservative. Test independent.
E4
Ignoring cycle position
Forecast at peak: extrapolate above-cycle margins → overestimate. Forecast at trough: extrapolate below-cycle → underestimate. Anchor margin to mid-cycle for cyclical company.
E5
Mismatched IS-BS-CF
Project revenue +20% mà capex flat — capacity contradiction. Project net income increase mà cash decreasing — working capital build hidden. Integrated model catches inconsistency.
E6
Ignore competitive response
Forecast company gain market share without modeling how competitors react. In reality, competitor will respond (price cut, product launch, M&A defense). Assume rational competitor.

Revenue forecast pick.

§ Vignette
An analyst is building a 5-year revenue forecast for a Vietnamese cement producer. Industry consultant data shows national cement consumption growing at 6% annually with the company's market share stable at 12%. The company sells one principal product (Portland cement) priced in VND/ton with no significant geographic mix changes. The most appropriate revenue forecast method is:
  1. ABottom-up segment build-up — sum forecasts for each individual customer to capture company-specific sales pipeline.
  2. BTop-down market-share approach combined with volume × price decomposition — industry consumption × stable market share gives volume; track ASP separately for price effect (e.g., inflation).
  3. CTrend extrapolation of last 5 years' revenue using simple regression, ignoring industry data because past performance fully reflects future trajectory.

Practice · Question 02 · Original

Operating leverage.

§ Vignette
Saigon Hotels reports the following income statement: Revenue 1,000B; Variable costs 200B; Fixed costs 600B; EBIT 200B. An analyst forecasts Year 2 revenue will rise 20% to 1,200B. Variable costs are expected to scale linearly with revenue; fixed costs remain at 600B. What is the most accurate EBIT forecast for Year 2?
  1. A240B — EBIT scales proportionally with revenue, so 20% revenue growth = 20% EBIT growth.
  2. B360B — Variable cost scales to 240B (20% increase), fixed cost stays at 600B; EBIT = 1,200 − 240 − 600 = 360B (80% EBIT growth from 200 to 360).
  3. C280B — Variable cost stays flat at 200B because the company has scale economies; only fixed cost rises with inflation.

Spot the leverage.

§ Keywords Variable cost scale linear + fixed cost remains + 20% revenue growth. Operating leverage tại work.

  2. Hint 2 Fixed cost stays at 600B (per assumption — capacity unchanged).
  3. Hint 3 EBIT Year 2 = 1,200 − 240 − 600 = 360B. EBIT growth = 360/200 − 1 = 80% trên revenue growth 20%. Đây là operating leverage = 4× (80%/20%).

Answer · Question 02

Answer: B — 360B.

✓ Correct — B
Step 1: Variable cost ratio Year 1 = 200/1,000 = 20% revenue. Step 2: Year 2 variable cost = 20% × 1,200 = 240B. Step 3: Fixed cost stays 600B per assumption. Step 4: EBIT Year 2 = 1,200 − 240 − 600 = 360B. EBIT growth = 360/200 − 1 = 80%, while revenue growth chỉ 20% → operating leverage 4×. Đây là illustration classic: high fixed cost (600/1000 = 60% revenue) khuếch đại revenue change → margin change. Saigon Hotels = high operating leverage business (most hotels). Ngược lại, dịch chuyển revenue 20% downward sẽ collapse EBIT từ 200 → 40B (−80%).
✗ Wrong — A
Sai cốt lõi — EBIT KHÔNG scale tỉ lệ với revenue khi có fixed cost. 20% revenue growth tạo EBIT growth lớn hơn (vì fixed cost stays). "Proportional scaling" chỉ đúng nếu tất cả costs là variable (rare). Đây là common forecasting error #5 — ignore operating leverage.
✗ Wrong — C
Sai vì variable cost đã được definition là scaling với revenue (per question setup). "Scale economies" không relevant — đó là argument cho cost structure shifting (fixed → variable hoặc vice versa), không phải variable cost flattening tự dưng. Câu này C misreads question.
Key takeaway
Operating leverage = (% Δ EBIT) / (% Δ Revenue). High fixed cost → high leverage → small revenue change → big EBIT change (cả 2 chiều). Cho hotels, airlines, factories, software — phải forecast carefully because extrapolating margin linearly với revenue sẽ sai. Build cost separately into variable + fixed.

Spot the error.

§ Vignette
An analyst's 5-year forecast for a Vietnamese airline shows: revenue growing at the airline's 5-year historical CAGR of 18% per year (which spanned 2019-2024 covering pandemic recovery); EBIT margin expanding from current 8% to 22% by Year 5 (vs the current best-in-class global airline operating at 14%); capex flat at depreciation level despite revenue tripling. Which is the most likely issue with this forecast?
  1. ANo issues — the forecast is internally consistent and grounded in observable historical data, with clear margin expansion and disciplined capex.
  2. BMultiple issues: (i) extrapolating recovery-period CAGR ignores cycle position, (ii) margin assumption exceeds best-in-class without unique advantage, (iii) capex flat vs revenue tripling implies impossible capacity utilization.
  3. COnly one issue — the capex is too low; revenue and margin assumptions are reasonable based on the company's historical performance.

Answer: B — 3 issues.

✓ Correct — B
Forecast có 3 separate red flags: (i) Cycle position ignored — 5Y CAGR 18% bao gồm 2020 pandemic collapse + 2021-2024 recovery. Extrapolating recovery-rate forward = assume permanent acceleration.
