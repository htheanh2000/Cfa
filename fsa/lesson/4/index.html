Third statement role

Why cash flow matters.

Income statement = profit (accrual basis). Balance sheet = position at point in time. Cash flow statement = actual cash movements trong period — explain how cash position changed. "Profit là opinion; cash là fact" — không có CF statement, không thể assess liquidity, solvency, hoặc earnings quality.

α
Profit ≠ Cash
Revenue ghi nhận khi earned (accrual), không khi cash collected. Expense ghi khi incurred, không khi cash paid. Gap = working capital changes + non-cash items.
β
Solvency lens
Profitable companies vẫn fail vì cash crunch. CF statement reveal liquidity status — đủ cash để pay bills, debt, dividend?
γ
Earnings quality check
CFO/NI ≥ 1 sustained = high quality (cash backs reported earnings). CFO/NI < 1 sustained = red flag (accrual-driven earnings, may not last).
δ
Reconciliation engine
CF integrate IS với BS — show how net income + asset/liab changes produce ending cash. Disciplined model exposes hidden inconsistencies.

Two accounting bases

Cash basis vs accrual basis.

Cash basis
Recognition
Revenue khi cash collected. Expense khi cash paid.
Use case
Small businesses (US allows for sole proprietors). Personal finance.
Pros
Simple; phản ánh actual cash position trực tiếp.
Cons
Misleading cho period matching. Vd ship product Dec 2025, collect Jan 2026 → revenue ghi nhận Jan 2026, không match với COGS Dec 2025.
Accrual basis
Recognition
Revenue khi earned (delivered). Expense khi incurred (matched với revenue).
Use case
All public companies (IFRS + US GAAP required). All material businesses.
Pros
Better matching of revenue + expense → meaningful margin. Period comparable.
Cons
Cash position obscured. Cần CF statement để translate accrual income → cash impact.

§ Therefore: IS + BS = accrual story; CF = cash translation. Cả 3 statements together = complete financial picture. Drop bất kỳ statement nào = incomplete.

CF structure

Three sections.

CF chia 3 sections theo activity type. Sum 3 = Net Change in Cash, reconcile với BS Cash beginning vs ending. Memorize section content — exam test "which goes in which section".

§ CF structure
CFO — Cash from Operating activities (core business)
CFI — Cash from Investing activities (long-term assets)
CFF — Cash from Financing activities (debt + equity)
CFO + CFI + CFF = Net change in Cash
Beginning Cash + Net Change = Ending Cash — reconcile với Balance Sheet Cash. CFO is the most-watched section (operating health). CFI shows reinvestment + divestment. CFF shows capital structure changes.

Section 1 · CFO

CFO — Operating activities.

§ CFO contents Inflows: Cash from customers (sales), interest received (US GAAP — see classification slide), dividends received (US GAAP).
Outflows: Cash to suppliers (COGS, inventory purchases), cash to employees (salary), cash for operating expenses (rent, utilities), interest paid (US GAAP), taxes paid.
Excludes: Cash from selling long-term assets (CFI), cash from issuing debt/equity (CFF), dividends paid (CFF for US GAAP).

§ CFO is the "heartbeat" — sustained healthy CFO = company can fund operations + reinvestment + financing without external help. Negative CFO sustained = warning sign (cash burn).

§ Quality framework CFO > NI consistently → high earnings quality.
CFO < NI sustained → accrual-driven earnings, possible quality issue.
Negative CFO + Positive NI = red flag (working capital build hidden in receivables / inventory; channel-stuffing risk).

Section 2 · CFI

CFI — Investing activities.

§ CFI contents Outflows (negative): Cash for capex (PP&E, plant, equipment), cash for acquisitions of other businesses, cash for purchases of intangible assets, cash for investments in marketable securities (long-term).
Inflows (positive): Cash from sale of long-term assets, cash from sale of business units, cash from sale of investments, proceeds from collecting principal of loans receivable.
Excludes: Interest/dividend received (CFO under US GAAP; either CFO or CFI under IFRS). Short-term investments may be in operating depending on classification.

§ CFI usually negative cho growing company (buying assets). Positive CFI = signal divestment hoặc winding down. Compare CFI vs depreciation: if capex (in CFI) ≈ depreciation → maintenance mode; if capex > depreciation → growth mode.

Section 3 · CFF

CFF — Financing activities.

§ CFF contents Inflows: Cash from issuing debt (bond, loan), cash from issuing equity (IPO, secondary offering).
Outflows: Cash for debt repayment (principal only — interest is CFO under US GAAP), cash for share buybacks (treasury stock), dividends paid (US GAAP — IFRS allows either CFO or CFF).
Excludes: Interest paid (CFO under US GAAP). Non-cash transactions like stock issued for acquisition (footnote disclosure only).

§ CFF reveals capital structure dynamics. Sustained net positive CFF = relying on external funding (raising debt/equity). Sustained net negative CFF = returning cash to investors (dividend + buyback + debt paydown). Mature companies typically negative CFF; growth companies positive.

Classification differences

IFRS vs US GAAP.

Same items classified different sections theo IFRS vs US GAAP. CFA exam test thẳng — memorize chính xác.

§ Classification matrix
Interest paid: US GAAP = CFO · IFRS = CFO or CFF
Interest received: US GAAP = CFO · IFRS = CFO or CFI
Dividends received: US GAAP = CFO · IFRS = CFO or CFI
Dividends paid: US GAAP = CFF · IFRS = CFO or CFF
Taxes paid: US GAAP = CFO · IFRS = CFO (default) or CFI/CFF if specifically identified
Memorize US GAAP: tất cả interest + dividends received + taxes vào CFO. Chỉ dividends paid vào CFF. IFRS flexible: phân loại theo policy choice nhưng phải nhất quán across periods.

§ Common exam trap — assume interest paid là CFF (giống debt repayment). Sai dưới US GAAP. Interest paid là operating theo US GAAP, nhưng dưới IFRS có thể được chọn CFF (logic là interest = financing cost).

CFO preparation · Method 1

Direct method.

Direct method shows actual cash receipts and payments. Bắt đầu với revenue/expense items từ IS, convert thành cash equivalents bằng cách điều chỉnh working capital changes.

§ Direct method CFO formula
Cash from customers = Revenue − ΔAR + ΔUnearned Revenue
Cash to suppliers = COGS − ΔInventory + ΔAP (− sign)
Cash to employees = Salary expense − ΔAccrued Salaries
Cash for operating expense = Op Exp − ΔPrepaid Exp − ΔAccrued Liab
Cash for taxes = Tax expense − ΔDeferred Tax + ΔTax Payable
CFO = Cash from customers − Cash to suppliers − Cash to employees − Cash for op exp − Cash for taxes
Direct method preferred theo IFRS + US GAAP (strongly recommended). Trên thực tế, < 5% companies dùng direct method vì heavier work + reveal more granular detail than mgmt prefer.

Direct method · numerical example

Direct method example.

§ Setup Revenue = 1,000B. AR start = 100B; AR end = 150B (ΔAR = +50B). Cash from customers = 1,000 − 50 = 950B.
COGS = 600B. Inventory: start 80B, end 120B (ΔInv = +40B). AP: start 60B, end 90B (ΔAP = +30B). Cash to suppliers = 600 + 40 − 30 = 610B.
Operating expenses = 200B. Accrued liab: start 20B, end 35B (ΔAccrued = +15B). Cash for op exp = 200 − 15 = 185B.
Tax expense = 50B. Tax payable: start 10B, end 8B (ΔPayable = −2B). Cash for taxes = 50 + 2 = 52B.
§ CFO calculation
CFO = 950 − 610 − 185 − 52 = 103B
Sanity check: Net Income = Revenue − COGS − OpEx − Tax = 1,000 − 600 − 200 − 50 = 150B. Difference NI − CFO = 150 − 103 = 47B = ΔAR (50) + ΔInv (40) − ΔAP (30) − ΔAccrued (15) + ΔTax Payable (2) = 47B ✓.

CFO preparation · Method 2

Indirect method.

Indirect method = start với Net Income, adjust để arrive at CFO. Adjustments: (a) add back non-cash items, (b) reverse gains/losses từ investing/financing items, (c) adjust for working capital changes.

§ Indirect method recipe
Net Income
+ Depreciation & Amortization (non-cash)
+ Stock-based compensation (non-cash)
+ Deferred tax expense (non-cash portion)
− Gain on sale of assets (move to CFI; gain is non-cash within CFO)
+ Loss on sale of assets
− Increase in AR / Inventory (cash tied up)
+ Decrease in AR / Inventory (cash freed)
+ Increase in AP / Accrued Liab (cash deferred)
− Decrease in AP / Accrued Liab
= CFO
Mnemonic: "Add non-cash, subtract gains, adjust working capital opposite of asset direction." Asset ↑ → cash ↓ (subtract). Liability ↑ → cash ↑ (add).

Indirect method · same example + comparison

Indirect arrives at same CFO.

§ Apply indirect to same data Net Income = 150B. ΔAR = +50B (cash tied). ΔInventory = +40B (cash tied). ΔAP = +30B (cash freed). ΔAccrued Liab = +15B (cash freed). ΔTax Payable = −2B (cash used). Assume no D&A or other non-cash for simplicity.
§ Indirect CFO calculation
NI = 150
− ΔAR = −50
− ΔInventory = −40
+ ΔAP = +30
+ ΔAccrued Liab = +15
− ΔTax Payable = −2
CFO = 150 − 50 − 40 + 30 + 15 − 2 = 103B ✓
Same answer như direct method. Direct method shows source of cash (revenue, expense detail); indirect method shows reconciliation (NI vs CFO gap). Indirect is preferred trên thực tế (~95% companies).

Statement integration

Linkages — IS · BS · CF.

3 statements không độc lập — chúng integrate. CF statement là "bridge" giữa IS (period activity) và BS (point in time). Disciplined model integration exposes inconsistency.

  1. IS → CF Net Income (from IS) → starting point CFO (indirect method). D&A expense (IS) → add back trong CFO. Gain/loss on asset sale (IS) → reverse trong CFO, move to CFI.
  2. BS Δ → CF adjustments Working capital changes (ΔAR, ΔInv, ΔAP) → CFO adjustments. ΔPP&E (capex) → CFI. ΔDebt, ΔEquity, ΔRE (via dividend) → CFF.
  3. CF → BS Cash Net change in cash (sum CFO + CFI + CFF) reconcile với BS Cash beginning vs ending. Validation: nếu không match, có error trong model. Most common error: missed non-cash item hoặc dual-counted item.
  4. RE roll-forward Retained Earnings end = RE begin + Net Income (IS) − Dividends declared. Dividends paid (CFF) ≠ dividends declared if there's a payable lag. Non-trivial cho companies với accrued dividends.

Non-cash transactions

Non-cash transactions disclosure.

§ Material non-cash transactions Vì không cash liên quan, các transactions sau KHÔNG xuất hiện trong CF body. Phải disclose trong footnote hoặc supplementary schedule.
N1
Stock-for-acquisition
Acquire target by issuing acquirer's shares. Vd merger paid 100% in stock. No cash movement → not in CF body, but disclosed in footnote.
N2
Debt-for-asset swap
Acquire equipment by issuing note payable directly to vendor. No cash exchange → not in CF body.
N3
Convertible debt to equity
Bondholders convert bonds → equity. Reduces debt, increases equity, no cash. Footnote.
N4
Lease arrangements
Capitalize finance lease → recognize asset + liability. No initial cash, but subsequent lease payments split CFO (interest portion under US GAAP) + CFF (principal).

Recap · big picture

Key takeaways.

α
3 sections + reconcile to BS Cash
CFO (operating) + CFI (investing) + CFF (financing) = Net Change in Cash. Begin Cash + Net Change = End Cash, must reconcile to BS.
β
IFRS flexible · US GAAP rigid
Memorize US GAAP: interest paid + interest received + dividends received → CFO. Dividends paid → CFF. IFRS allows interest paid → CFO or CFF; dividends paid → CFO or CFF; interest/div received → CFO or CFI.
γ
Direct vs Indirect — same CFO
Direct = revenue/expense → cash items (granular). Indirect = NI + adjustments. Indirect dominant in practice (95%+). Both arrive at same CFO.
δ
Non-cash transactions → footnote
Stock-for-acquisition, debt-for-asset, convertible conversion, lease capitalization. No cash → not in CF body, must disclose in footnote.

Interest paid classification.

§ Vignette
A US-listed company paid $50 million in interest on outstanding bonds during the year. Under US GAAP, where must this $50 million appear in the cash flow statement?
  1. ACash flow from operating activities (CFO) — interest paid is treated as an operating outflow under US GAAP.
  2. BCash flow from financing activities (CFF) — interest is a financing cost related to debt, similar to dividends paid which are also CFF.
  3. CEither CFO or CFF — the company can choose its preferred classification, similar to IFRS flexibility.

Hint · Question 01

Spot the standard.

§ Memorize US GAAP rigid: interest paid + interest/div received → CFO; dividends paid → CFF. IFRS flexible: choose policy.
  1. Hint 1 US GAAP treats interest expense as operating cost (vì interest goes through IS as expense reducing operating income). Hence cash payment for interest = CFO.
  2. Hint 2 IFRS more logical (interest = financing) — allows interest paid in either CFO or CFF. Nhưng đề specify US GAAP → no flexibility.
  3. Hint 3 Common trap (đáp án B): logically interest should be financing, but US GAAP rule treats it as CFO. Memorize the rule, don't reason from logic.

Answer · Question 01

Answer: A — CFO.

✓ Correct — A
US GAAP requires interest paid be classified as CFO. Logic: interest expense flows through IS as part of net income (reducing operating earnings); the corresponding cash payment is treated as operating outflow to maintain consistency với IS. Cùng quy tắc cho interest received + dividends received (both CFO under US GAAP). Only dividends paid goes to CFF under US GAAP. Memorize: US GAAP rigid classification — không có "company can choose".
✗ Wrong — B
Logically tempting (interest is a financing cost related to debt), nhưng US GAAP không follow economic logic — rule placed interest paid trong CFO. Đây là common conceptual trap. Note: under IFRS, B would be acceptable (IFRS allows CFO or CFF), but question specify US GAAP.
✗ Wrong — C
Sai cốt lõi — chỉ IFRS cho phép policy choice; US GAAP rigid. Đảo ngược nguyên tắc. Memorize matrix: US GAAP single classification per item; IFRS allows election (must be consistent).
Key takeaway
US GAAP CF classification matrix: Interest paid → CFO; Interest received → CFO; Dividends received → CFO; Dividends paid → CFF; Taxes paid → CFO. IFRS allows election cho interest/dividends (CFO or CFF/CFI). Memorize US GAAP rigid rules — exam tests verbatim.

Practice · Question 02 · Original

Indirect method CFO.

§ Vignette
A company reports the following data for the year (in millions): Net Income = 200; Depreciation = 80; Loss on sale of equipment = 15; Increase in accounts receivable = 40; Decrease in inventory = 25; Increase in accounts payable = 30; Decrease in accrued liabilities = 10. What is the cash flow from operating activities using the indirect method?
  1. A280 — calculated as 200 + 80 + 15 − 40 + 25 + 30 + 10 (all working capital changes added).
  2. B300 — calculated as 200 + 80 + 15 − 40 + 25 + 30 − 10 (correct treatment: add non-cash, subtract gain, AR ↑ subtract, Inv ↓ add, AP ↑ add, Accrued ↓ subtract).
  3. C240 — calculated as 200 + 80 − 15 + 40 − 25 − 30 + 10 (subtract loss, then reverse all working capital adjustments).

Hint · Question 02

Spot the signs.

§ Recipe Indirect = NI + non-cash + losses − gains + (Asset ↓ → +) + (Liab ↑ → +). Asset ↑ → −. Liab ↓ → −.
  1. Hint 1 Non-cash items: Depreciation +80 (always add back). Loss on sale +15 (add back; would be subtracted if it were a gain).
  2. Hint 2 Working capital — Asset side: AR ↑ 40 → cash tied up → −40. Inventory ↓ 25 → cash freed → +25.
  3. Hint 3 Working capital — Liability side: AP ↑ 30 → cash deferred → +30. Accrued Liab ↓ 10 → cash paid → −10. Sum: 200 + 80 + 15 − 40 + 25 + 30 − 10 = 300.

Practice · Question 02 · Tiếng Việt

Dịch đề.

§ Vignette
Một công ty báo cáo dữ liệu cả năm (triệu): Net Income = 200; Depreciation = 80; Loss on sale of equipment = 15; Increase in AR = 40; Decrease in inventory = 25; Increase in AP = 30; Decrease in accrued liabilities = 10. Cash flow from operating activities theo indirect method là?
  1. A280 — tính bằng 200 + 80 + 15 − 40 + 25 + 30 + 10 (cộng hết working capital changes).
  2. B300 — tính bằng 200 + 80 + 15 − 40 + 25 + 30 − 10 (đúng: cộng non-cash, trừ gain, AR ↑ trừ, Inv ↓ cộng, AP ↑ cộng, Accrued ↓ trừ).
  3. C240 — tính bằng 200 + 80 − 15 + 40 − 25 − 30 + 10 (trừ loss, sau đó đảo ngược tất cả working capital adjustments).

Answer · Question 02

Answer: B — 300.

✓ Correct — B
Apply indirect method recipe step-by-step: (1) Start NI = 200. (2) Add D&A = +80 (non-cash, always add). (3) Add loss on sale = +15 (loss reduce NI but no cash; if gain, would subtract). (4) AR ↑ 40 → cash tied in receivables → −40. (5) Inventory ↓ 25 → cash freed (sold inventory) → +25. (6) AP ↑ 30 → cash deferred (delaying payment to suppliers) → +30. (7) Accrued Liab ↓ 10 → paid down accrued obligations → −10. Sum: 200 + 80 + 15 − 40 + 25 + 30 − 10 = 300.
✗ Wrong — A
Sai phần Accrued Liab — Accrued Liab ↓ means company paid down → cash out → must subtract −10, không add +10. Adding all working capital changes ignores direction of asset vs liability.
✗ Wrong — C
Multiple errors: (1) loss should be added (+15), not subtracted; loss reduce NI but no actual cash outflow. (2) Working capital signs reversed — AR ↑ should be −, not +. Indirect recipe requires understanding direction of cash flow vs balance sheet movement.
Key takeaway
Indirect method 4 sign rules: (1) Non-cash items → add (D&A, SBC, deferred tax, losses); (2) Gains → subtract (gain reflected in NI but cash is in CFI); (3) Asset ↑ → subtract, Asset ↓ → add (working capital tied/freed); (4) Liability ↑ → add, Liability ↓ → subtract (cash deferred/paid). Memorize 4 rules → solve any indirect CFO problem.

Direct method — cash from customers.

§ Vignette
A company reports Revenue of $5,000 million. Beginning AR = $400 million; ending AR = $550 million. Beginning Unearned Revenue = $80 million; ending Unearned Revenue = $120 million. Using the direct method, what is the cash collected from customers for the year?
  1. A$4,810M — calculated as Revenue − ΔAR + ΔUnearned = 5,000 − 150 + (−40) = 4,810.
  2. B$4,890M — calculated as Revenue − ΔAR + ΔUnearned = 5,000 − 150 + 40 = 4,890.
  3. C$5,150M — calculated as Revenue + ΔAR + ΔUnearned = 5,000 + 150 + 40 = 5,190 (and adjusted slightly).

Spot the signs.

§ Formula Cash from customers = Revenue − ΔAR + ΔUnearned Revenue
  1. Hint 1 ΔAR = 550 − 400 = +150 (AR increased). Cash not yet collected went up by 150 → subtract 150 from revenue (cash less than revenue earned).

Answer · Question 03

Answer: B — $4,890M.

✓ Correct — B
Direct method formula: Cash from customers = Revenue − ΔAR + ΔUnearned Revenue. Step 1: ΔAR = 550 − 400 = +150 (AR ↑ → less cash collected, subtract). Step 2: ΔUnearned Revenue = 120 − 80 = +40 (Unearned ↑ → more cash received in advance, add). Step 3: 5,000 − 150 + 40 = 4,890. Intuition: revenue ghi nhận khi earned. Nếu AR ↑, có thêm revenue earned chưa collected → cash < revenue. Nếu Unearned ↑, có thêm cash collected chưa earn → cash > revenue. Kết hợp: cả 2 effects net.
✗ Wrong — A
Sai sign trên ΔUnearned — đề nói Unearned tăng từ 80 → 120 (positive +40), không phải −40. Nếu trừ −40 = subtract negative = effectively adding 40 (giống đáp án B), nhưng arithmetic ghi −40 là wrong notation. ΔUnearned = +40 (positive change).
✗ Wrong — C
Sai sign trên ΔAR — AR ↑ should be subtracted (cash tied up in receivables). Adding ΔAR overstates cash collected. Logic: AR là claim chưa collected; AR ↑ = cash hasn't been received, must subtract from revenue.
Key takeaway
Direct method cash from customers = Revenue − ΔAR + ΔUnearned Revenue. Memorize signs: AR ↑ tied cash (subtract); Unearned ↑ cash received early (add). Same logic applies to other line items: Cash to suppliers = COGS + ΔInv − ΔAP; Cash to employees = Salary − ΔAccrued Salary; Cash for taxes = Tax expense − ΔDeferred Tax + ΔTax Payable.
