Beyond preparation

Why analyze CF?

Preparation (m04) tells what numbers are. Analysis (m05) tells what numbers mean. CF analysis answer 4 questions: (1) Is CFO healthy + sustainable? (2) Where is cash going? (3) Can company cover obligations? (4) Is reported earnings backed by cash?

α
Source & use of cash
Identify primary source (CFO operations? CFI divestment? CFF debt?). Identify primary use (capex growth? debt paydown? dividend?). Match story tells thesis.
β
Sustainability lens
CF healthy = company can self-fund. Reliant on external financing = vulnerable to credit cycle. Free cash flow excess = optionality (dividend, buyback, M&A, debt paydown).
γ
Solvency assessment
CF coverage ratios determine ability service debt + interest. Bond analyst, credit rating agency rely heavily on CF metrics, không chỉ income.
δ
Earnings quality verification
CFO/NI is the headline quality ratio. Track over time + compare peer. Sustained < 1 = warning; >= 1 = high quality.

Common-size analysis

Common-size CF.

Express each CF line item as % of some base. Two methods phổ biến: % of revenue (CFO line) hoặc % of total cash inflow / outflow. Make companies of different size comparable + reveal trend over time.

Method 1 — % of Revenue
Application
Each CF line / Revenue. Vd CFO/Rev, Capex/Rev, FCF/Rev. Compare across companies of different size. Compare across years.
Useful for
Operating efficiency benchmark. Capex intensity benchmark. FCF margin track.
Example
Software firm: CFO/Rev typically 30-40%. Industrial: 8-15%. Restaurant: 6-10%. Trend ↑ = improving cash conversion.
Method 2 — % of Total Cash Flows
Application
Inflows: each inflow / total inflow. Outflows: each outflow / total outflow. Reveal source & use mix.
Useful for
Identify primary source of cash: CFO 80% + CFI 5% + CFF 15% = healthy operating-funded. CFO 20% + CFF 70% = debt-dependent.
Example
Mature: CFO ~ 100% of inflow; outflows split capex 40% + dividend 30% + buyback 20% + debt 10%.

Free cash flow · FCFF

FCFF — Free Cash Flow to Firm.

FCFF = cash flow available to all capital providers (debt + equity holders) sau khi trang trải operating expenses + working capital + capex. Foundation cho enterprise valuation (DCF firm-level).

§ FCFF formulas (3 equivalent paths)
FCFF = CFO + Interest × (1 − tax) − Capex
FCFF = NI + NCC + Interest × (1 − tax) − ΔWC − Capex
FCFF = EBIT × (1 − tax) + NCC − ΔWC − Capex
NCC = Non-Cash Charges (D&A, deferred tax, etc.). Interest × (1 − tax) = after-tax interest. Khi start từ CFO, must add back (interest đã treat as CFO under US GAAP, but FCFF excludes financing). Khi start từ EBIT, không cần add (EBIT pre-interest).

§ Why add back interest? Vì FCFF dành cho both debt + equity providers — interest đã "captured" cho debt holders, nhưng FCFF đo cash available to ALL providers before distributing to either.

Free cash flow · FCFE

FCFE — Free Cash Flow to Equity.

FCFE = cash flow available to equity holders only sau khi pay debt obligations (interest + principal). Foundation cho equity valuation (DCF equity-level, dividend discount model).

§ FCFE formulas (2 equivalent paths)
FCFE = CFO − Capex + Net Borrowing
FCFE = FCFF − Interest × (1 − tax) + Net Borrowing
Net Borrowing = New debt issued − Debt repaid. Positive = debt finance helps cash; negative = debt paydown drains cash. Add back vì equity holders benefit khi company can borrow.
§ FCFF vs FCFE — when to use which FCFF: dùng khi capital structure changing hoặc uncertain (vd LBO target, growth company adjusting leverage). Discount với WACC.
FCFE: dùng khi capital structure stable (mature company). Discount với cost of equity (Re). FCFE simpler nhưng less robust khi leverage changing.

CF performance ratios

Cash performance ratios.

§ Performance ratios
Cash flow to revenue = CFO / Revenue
Cash return on assets = CFO / Avg Total Assets
Cash return on equity = CFO / Avg Common Equity
Cash to income = CFO / Operating Income
Cash flow per share = (CFO − Preferred Dividends) / Weighted Avg Shares
Same logic như profitability ratios (ROA, ROE, profit margin) nhưng với CFO numerator thay vì NI. Rationale: cash là "harder" measure than accrual income.

§ Compare CFO/Rev với Net Margin (NI/Rev). Sustained CFO/Rev < Net Margin = quality issue (NI inflated by accruals). CFO/Rev > Net Margin = high quality (cash exceeds reported earnings, often due to D&A non-cash + working capital release).

CF coverage ratios

Cash coverage ratios.

§ Coverage ratios
Debt coverage = CFO / Total Debt
Interest coverage = (CFO + Interest paid + Taxes paid) / Interest paid
Reinvestment coverage = CFO / Capex
Dividend coverage = CFO / Dividends paid
Investing & financing coverage = CFO / (Capex + Dividends + Debt repayment)
≥ 1 = CFO covers obligation. < 1 sustained = relying on external funding to cover. Bond analysts focus on Debt coverage + Interest coverage as key solvency metrics.

§ Note interest coverage uses (CFO + Interest + Taxes) ÷ Interest — vì CFO is after interest paid (under US GAAP), need to add back interest + taxes to get cash available before financing. Tương tự EBIT-based interest coverage on income statement.

Quality assessment

Quality of cash flow.

"High quality CFO" = sustainable, recurring, derived from core operations. "Low quality CFO" = boosted by one-offs, working capital release, accounting choices. Quality ≠ size.

Q1
Sustainable source
CFO from recurring operations is high quality. CFO boosted by one-time items (litigation settlement, asset sale gain reclassified) is low quality. Investigate footnote.
Q2
Working capital release ≠ recurring
Pulling DSO faster, cutting inventory, stretching DPO can spike CFO temporarily — but limit. Cannot keep stretching forever. CFO boost từ working capital is low quality (one-time).
Q3
CFO > NI sustained = good
D&A contribute (non-cash). Healthy WC management. Ratio stable above 1 across cycle.
Q4
CFO < NI sustained = bad
Earnings driven by accruals. Receivables building, inventory bloating. Likely write-down or earnings reset coming.

CF profile · company stage

CF profile by company stage.

§ CF signature by stage
Startup: CFO − · CFI − · CFF + (cash burn, heavy capex, equity/debt funding)
Growth: CFO + · CFI − · CFF + or − (positive ops, heavy capex, funding still needed)
Mature: CFO + + · CFI − · CFF − (strong ops fund capex + return to investors)
Decline: CFO + or − · CFI + · CFF − (asset sales, debt paydown, dividend cut)
Pattern − − + = startup. + − − = mature. + + − hoặc − + − = decline (asset divestment + debt paydown). Detect mismatch giữa stated strategy & actual signature → flag.

§ Investment implication — mature company với startup signature (− − +) = warning sign (operations deteriorating, requires external funding). Startup với mature signature (+ − −) = unusual and bullish (strong ops + returning cash).

Warning signs

CF red flags.

R1
CFO < NI persistent
Earnings không backed bằng cash. Accruals building. Investigate AR + inventory + accrued reserves. Possible channel-stuffing, aggressive revenue recognition.
R2
CFO declining + NI rising
Divergence widening. Definite quality issue. Earnings being inflated, cash collapsing.
R3
Capex < Depreciation chronic
Under-investing in maintenance. Asset base eroding. Eventually capacity constraint or product obsolescence. Sustainable only short-term.
R4
Working capital release boosting CFO
CFO spike từ DPO stretching, inventory cut. One-time gain. Not repeatable. Track CFO ex-WC change cho true operating CF.
R5
Reclassifying items between sections
Mid-year reclass capex from CFI to CFO (or vice versa). Boost CFO artificially. Read footnote for change in classification.
R6
Heavy reliance on CFF
CFO insufficient → constant debt issuance / equity raise. Vulnerable khi credit cycle tightens. Not sustainable for mature company.

Recap · big picture

Key takeaways.

α
FCFF vs FCFE
FCFF = cash for all capital providers; discount with WACC. FCFE = cash for equity only; discount with Re. FCFE = FCFF − Interest(1−t) + Net Borrowing.
β
Performance + Coverage ratios
Performance: CFO/Revenue, CFO/Assets, CFO/Equity. Coverage: CFO/Debt, CFO/Capex, CFO/Dividends. ≥ 1 coverage = self-fund.
γ
CFO/NI quality test
CFO/NI ≥ 1 sustained = high quality. < 1 sustained = accrual-driven, red flag. Track over time + peer benchmark.
δ
Stage signature
Startup: − − +. Growth: + − ±. Mature: + − −. Decline: ± + −. Mismatch giữa stated stage & actual signature = flag.

Practice · Question 01 · Original

FCFF computation.

§ Vignette
A US-listed company reports the following data for the year (in millions): Cash Flow from Operations (CFO) = 500; Interest paid = 100; Tax rate = 25%; Capital Expenditures = 200; Net Borrowing (new debt − repayment) = 50. What is the company's Free Cash Flow to the Firm (FCFF)?
  1. A300 — calculated as CFO − Capex = 500 − 200 = 300.
  2. B375 — calculated as CFO + Interest × (1 − tax) − Capex = 500 + 100 × 0.75 − 200 = 500 + 75 − 200 = 375.
  3. C425 — calculated as CFO + Interest paid − Capex + Net Borrowing = 500 + 100 − 200 + 50 = 450 (and adjusted slightly).

04.05 — CF Part II 15 / 28
Hint · Question 01

Spot the add-back.

§ Formula FCFF = CFO + Interest × (1 − tax) − Capex. Note: after-tax interest, not pre-tax.
  1. Hint 1 CFO under US GAAP is after interest paid (interest treated as CFO). FCFF dành cho all capital providers (debt + equity), should be before any payments to providers → add interest back.
  2. Hint 2 Interest paid was tax-deductible, so true cash impact = Interest × (1 − tax). Adding back full pre-tax interest would overstate FCFF.
  3. Hint 3 Net Borrowing relevant cho FCFE (equity holders), KHÔNG cho FCFF (firm-level). FCFF formula does not use Net Borrowing.

Dịch đề.

§ Vignette
Một công ty niêm yết US báo cáo dữ liệu cả năm (triệu): CFO = 500; Interest paid = 100; Tax rate = 25%; Capital Expenditures = 200; Net Borrowing (new debt − repayment) = 50. Free Cash Flow to the Firm (FCFF) của công ty là?
  1. A300 — tính bằng CFO − Capex = 500 − 200 = 300.
  2. B375 — tính bằng CFO + Interest × (1 − tax) − Capex = 500 + 100 × 0.75 − 200 = 500 + 75 − 200 = 375.
  3. C425 — tính bằng CFO + Interest paid − Capex + Net Borrowing = 500 + 100 − 200 + 50 = 450 (đã adjust nhẹ).

Answer · Question 01

Answer: B — 375.

✓ Correct — B
FCFF = CFO + Interest × (1 − tax) − Capex = 500 + 100 × 0.75 − 200 = 500 + 75 − 200 = 375. Logic: CFO under US GAAP đã trừ interest paid (interest classified as CFO). FCFF dành cho all capital providers — phải add interest back để get cash before any payment to providers. Add back chỉ after-tax portion vì interest tax-deductible (tax shield đã capture trong CFO). Nếu add full pre-tax 100 → overstate FCFF by 25 (the tax saved).
✗ Wrong — A
Sai cốt lõi — bỏ qua add-back interest. CFO − Capex là FCFE phổ biến approximation, không phải FCFF. FCFF cần thêm after-tax interest add-back. Khi tax rate = 0%, A = B; nhưng khi tax positive, B luôn cao hơn A.
✗ Wrong — C
Multiple errors: (1) added pre-tax interest (100 instead of 75) — overstates by tax shield. (2) Net Borrowing không thuộc FCFF formula (đó là FCFE input). (3) Arithmetic mismatched (says 450 but lists 425). Đáp án mix FCFF + FCFE inputs.
Key takeaway
FCFF formula 3 paths: (1) CFO + Int(1−t) − Capex; (2) NI + NCC + Int(1−t) − ΔWC − Capex; (3) EBIT(1−t) + NCC − ΔWC − Capex. Khi start từ CFO hoặc NI, must add Int(1−t). Khi start từ EBIT (pre-interest), không cần. FCFE = FCFF − Int(1−t) + Net Borrowing.

Practice · Question 02 · Original

Interest coverage.

§ Vignette
A company reports CFO of $400 million, interest paid of $80 million, and taxes paid of $120 million during the year. The company is US GAAP-compliant. What is the cash flow interest coverage ratio?
  1. A5.0× — calculated as CFO / Interest = 400 / 80 = 5.0.
  2. B7.5× — calculated as (CFO + Interest paid + Taxes paid) / Interest paid = (400 + 80 + 120) / 80 = 600 / 80 = 7.5.
  3. C6.0× — calculated as (CFO + Interest paid) / Interest paid = (400 + 80) / 80 = 480 / 80 = 6.0.

Hint · Question 02

Spot the add-backs.

§ Formula Interest coverage (CF basis) = (CFO + Interest paid + Taxes paid) / Interest paid
  1. Hint 1 CFO under US GAAP is after interest paid + taxes paid. To compute "cash available before financing/tax obligations", add both back.
  2. Hint 2 Numerator = (400 + 80 + 120) = 600. Denominator = 80. Ratio = 600/80 = 7.5×. Interpretation: company generated 7.5× the interest needed.
  3. Hint 3 Đáp án A (CFO/Int) bỏ qua add-back. Đáp án C add interest only without taxes. Both miss key adjustment vì US GAAP CFO already deducted both.

§ Vignette
Một công ty báo cáo CFO $400 triệu, interest paid $80 triệu, taxes paid $120 triệu trong năm. Công ty US GAAP-compliant. Cash flow interest coverage ratio là?
  1. A5.0× — tính bằng CFO / Interest = 400 / 80 = 5.0.
  2. B7.5× — tính bằng (CFO + Interest paid + Taxes paid) / Interest paid = (400 + 80 + 120) / 80 = 600 / 80 = 7.5.
  3. C6.0× — tính bằng (CFO + Interest paid) / Interest paid = (400 + 80) / 80 = 480 / 80 = 6.0.

04.05 — CF Part II 21 / 28
Answer · Question 02

Answer: B — 7.5×.

✓ Correct — B
Cash flow interest coverage = (CFO + Interest paid + Taxes paid) / Interest paid = (400 + 80 + 120) / 80 = 600 / 80 = 7.5×. Logic: CFO under US GAAP đã deduct cả interest và taxes. Để measure "cash available trước any financing & tax obligation", phải add both back. Tử số = pre-interest, pre-tax operating cash. Mẫu = interest required to service debt. Ratio > 1 = cover được interest; 7.5× = comfortable cushion.
✗ Wrong — A
Sai cốt lõi — CFO/Int bỏ qua add-back của cả interest + taxes. Understate true cash available cho debt service. Sự khác biệt với EBIT-based interest coverage (EBIT/Int): EBIT pre-interest pre-tax; CFO post-both → cần add back.
✗ Wrong — C
Một nửa đúng — add back interest nhưng quên taxes. CFO is also after taxes, so taxes paid phải được add back as well để get pre-tax pre-interest cash. Common error → underestimate coverage.
Key takeaway
Cash flow interest coverage = (CFO + Interest paid + Taxes paid) / Interest paid. Logic: CFO post-interest post-tax under US GAAP, so add both back. Compare với EBIT-based interest coverage = EBIT / Interest paid (pre-tax, pre-interest natural cho IS-based formula). Both used by credit analysts; CF-based is harder to manipulate.

CF stage signature.

§ Vignette
A 30-year-old industrial conglomerate reports the following for the past 3 years: CFO consistently strongly positive ($600M, $650M, $700M); CFI consistently large negative (−$400M, −$420M, −$450M, mostly capex); CFF consistently negative (−$200M, −$230M, −$250M, mix of dividend + debt paydown + share buyback). Which life cycle stage signature does this most likely represent?
  1. AStartup stage — heavy investment in capex (CFI very negative) shows the company is still scaling its asset base.
  2. BMature stage — strong recurring CFO (positive growing) funds reinvestment (CFI negative) and returns to shareholders + debt paydown (CFF negative). Classic + − − pattern of mature, self-funding firm.
  3. CDecline stage — three years of negative CFI and CFF indicates the firm is winding down operations and returning cash to investors before exit.

Dịch đề.

§ Vignette
Một industrial conglomerate 30 tuổi báo cáo 3 năm gần đây: CFO consistently strongly positive ($600M, $650M, $700M); CFI consistently large negative (−$400M, −$420M, −$450M, chủ yếu capex); CFF consistently negative (−$200M, −$230M, −$250M, mix of dividend + debt paydown + share buyback). Stage life cycle nào nhiều khả năng represent signature này?
  1. AStartup stage — heavy investment trong capex (CFI rất âm) cho thấy công ty vẫn scaling asset base.
  2. BMature stage — CFO recurring mạnh (positive growing) fund reinvestment (CFI negative) và return to shareholders + debt paydown (CFF negative). Pattern + − − classic của mature self-funding firm.
  3. CDecline stage — 3 năm CFI và CFF negative cho thấy firm đang winding down operations và return cash trước exit.

Answer · Question 03

Answer: B — Mature.

✓ Correct — B
Pattern + − − = classic mature stage signature. (1) CFO strongly positive + growing = healthy recurring operations. (2) CFI negative from capex = continuing to reinvest in business but at sustainable rate. (3) CFF negative from dividend + buyback + debt paydown = returning cash to investors, no need for external funding. Self-funded. 30-year industrial age confirms stage. Mature company excellent profile cho dividend investor + bond investor (low solvency risk).
✗ Wrong — A
Sai pattern — startup signature là − − + (cash burn → CFO negative; heavy capex → CFI negative; raise funds → CFF positive). Đề có CFO positive và CFF negative, opposite of startup. Heavy capex alone không đủ để label startup; many mature companies có heavy maintenance capex.
✗ Wrong — C
Sai pattern — decline signature là CFI positive (asset divestments, sale of business units), không phải negative. Decline firms typically shrink asset base, not invest. CFI heavily negative confirms continued reinvestment, not winding down. Also CFO growing không phù hợp decline.
Key takeaway
CF stage signatures: Startup − − + (burn + capex + external funding); Growth + − ± (positive ops + heavy capex + funding still needed); Mature + − − (strong ops fund reinvestment + return); Decline ± + − (asset divestment + debt paydown). Read all 3 sections together; signature reveals stage even khi mgmt presents differently.
