Market context

Why Fixed Income matters.

Global bond market ~$140 trillion outstanding (2025), ~2× equity market. FI là core của institutional portfolios (pension, insurance, sovereign wealth) vì predictable cash flow + capital preservation. Hiểu FI = hiểu cách money flow giữa borrowers và lenders.

α
Predictable cash flow
Bonds promise defined payments (coupon + principal) at known dates. Equity dividends discretionary; bond payments contractual obligation.
β
Capital structure seniority
Bondholders rank above equity holders trong bankruptcy claim. Senior secured first → unsecured → subordinated → preferred → common equity. Lower risk, lower return.
γ
Diverse universe
Sovereign · supranational · municipal · corporate (IG/HY) · securitized (ABS/MBS) · structured. Cover credit risk spectrum AAA → CCC.
δ
Diversifier
Bonds traditionally negative-correlated với equities trong recession (rates ↓ as flight-to-quality). Foundation cho 60/40 portfolio classic.

Three core components

Bond anatomy.

Mọi bond có 3 components cốt lõi: par value (principal), coupon rate (interest), và maturity (term). 3 thông số này định hình bond's contractual cash flow profile.

  1. Par value (face value, principal) Amount issuer cam kết trả lại tại maturity. Standard $1,000 cho US corporate; $100 hoặc £100 cho some markets. Quoted price là % of par (e.g., 98.50 = 98.50% × par = $985 cho $1,000 par bond).
  2. Coupon rate Annual interest rate trên par. Coupon $ = Coupon rate × Par. Vd 5% × $1,000 = $50/năm. Payment frequency thường semi-annual US, annual Europe. Zero-coupon = no periodic interest, sold at deep discount.
  3. Maturity (term) Date issuer trả lại principal. Short-term < 1Y (T-bill), medium-term 1-10Y (T-note), long-term > 10Y (T-bond). Perpetual bond không có maturity. Original maturity vs remaining maturity (declining over time).
§ Quick example A 5-year semi-annual 6% coupon bond, par $1,000: pays $30 every 6 months (10 payments × $30 = $300 total interest) + $1,000 principal at maturity. Total cash to investor = $1,300 over 5 years if held to maturity.

Bond contract

The indenture.

Indenture (trust deed) = legal contract giữa issuer và bondholders. Specify all terms: payment schedule, collateral, covenants, default events, trustee duties. Trustee (usually bank) acts as fiduciary cho bondholders.

Affirmative covenants
Definition
Things issuer must do. Affirmative obligations.
Examples
Maintain minimum financial ratios (current ratio, debt/equity, interest coverage). File audited financials annually. Maintain insurance on collateral. Use proceeds for stated purpose.
Violation
Technical default. Trustee can declare acceleration (entire principal due immediately).
Negative covenants
Definition
Things issuer must not do. Restrictive covenants.
Examples
Cannot incur additional debt above limit. Cannot pay dividends above specified amount. Cannot sell major assets. Cannot merge without bondholder consent.
Purpose
Protect bondholders từ management actions that increase risk after bond issued (asset substitution, dividend recap, leveraged buyout).

§ Investment grade bonds thường có fewer/looser covenants (issuer financial strength reduces need). High yield bonds có extensive covenant package — bondholders need protection against more-risky issuers.

Issuer taxonomy

Five issuer types.

I1
Sovereign (government)
National governments — UST (US Treasury), Bunds (Germany), JGB (Japan), VGB (Vietnam). Generally lowest credit risk trong own country. Default rare for major DM; EM sovereigns occasionally default.
I2
Non-sovereign government
Sub-sovereign: states (US), provinces (Canada), cities (municipal bonds). Quasi-government: government agencies (Ginnie Mae), GSEs (Fannie/Freddie). Backed by issuing entity, not full sovereign.
I3
Supranational
Multinational entities: World Bank, IMF, ADB, EIB. Funded by member countries, lend to developing economies. Generally AAA rated.
I4
Corporate
Companies issuing debt. Investment grade (AAA-BBB) and high yield / junk (BB and below). Bank loans (private) vs corporate bonds (public). Hierarchy: senior secured → senior unsecured → subordinated.
I5
Securitized / Structured
ABS, MBS, CDO. Cash flow từ pool of underlying assets (mortgages, auto loans, credit card receivables). Special purpose vehicles (SPVs) issue bonds backed by specific asset pool, isolated from issuer credit risk.
I6
Banks & financial institutions
Special category — issue covered bonds, senior preferred, contingent convertibles (CoCo). Subject to bank regulation (Basel III). Coupon may be deferred trong stress (CoCo trigger).

Tax treatment

Tax considerations.

§ Three tax dimensions (1) Coupon income tax: Generally taxed as ordinary income in country of investor's residence. Rate matches investor's marginal income tax rate. Some bonds tax-exempt (vd US municipal bonds exempt from federal tax + state tax for in-state investors).
(2) Capital gains tax: If bond bought below par + held to maturity, $X gain at maturity may be taxed differently from coupon. Rules vary jurisdiction.
(3) Withholding tax: Some countries withhold tax từ foreign investors. Vd Vietnam withhold 5-10% trên coupon paid to foreign holders. Tax treaties can reduce.
§ Special tax-treated bonds US Municipal bonds: coupon tax-exempt federally; in-state often state tax-exempt too. Lower nominal yield reflects tax advantage.
UK Gilts: generally subject to income tax; some accrued interest treatment.
Zero-coupon bonds: "phantom income" — investor taxed annually on accrued interest dù chưa nhận cash. Issue cho tax-deferred account preferable.

§ Tax matters because after-tax yield là what investor actually keeps. Investor in 30% bracket comparing 5% taxable corporate vs 4% tax-exempt municipal: corporate after-tax = 5% × (1−0.3) = 3.5%; municipal = 4% (unchanged). Municipal wins despite lower nominal.

Currency & legal jurisdiction

Currency & jurisdiction.

Currency denomination
Domestic
Issuer + currency same. Vd Apple issuing USD bonds in US — domestic. Vietnam government issuing VND bond — domestic.
Foreign (Yankee, Samurai, Bulldog)
Foreign issuer in domestic market local currency. Yankee bond = non-US issuer trong USD market US. Samurai bond = non-Japanese issuer trong JPY market Japan. Bulldog = non-UK in GBP/UK.
Eurobond
Issued outside country of currency, không tied to single jurisdiction. Vd Eurodollar bond = USD bond issued in London. Reduce regulatory burden, attract international investors.
Dual-currency / FX-linked
Coupon in 1 currency, principal trong another. Or coupon linked to FX rate. Embedded FX exposure for both issuer and investor.
Legal jurisdiction
Indenture governing law
Specifies which country's law applies trong dispute. New York law + English law most common cho international bonds (well-developed bondholder protection precedent).
Listing exchange
Where bond listed for trading. Influences disclosure requirements, settlement, regulatory oversight.
Tax jurisdiction
Where issuer + investor tax-resident. Determines withholding + tax treatment.
Bankruptcy regime
Rules differ — US Chapter 11 vs English administration vs Vietnam corporate bankruptcy law. Affect recovery prospects.

Bond identifiers

Bond identifiers.

Mỗi bond cần unique identifier để track trong global trading systems. Three major systems used internationally — knowing which is which matter for cross-border trading.

§ Three bond identifiers
ISIN — International Securities Identification Number (12 char, global standard)
CUSIP — Committee on Uniform Securities Identification Procedures (9 char, US/Canada)
SEDOL — Stock Exchange Daily Official List (7 char, UK/Ireland)
ISIN is the global standard — accepted in all markets; first 2 chars country code. CUSIP primary in US — embedded in ISIN US-issued bonds (US + 9-char CUSIP + check digit = 12-char ISIN). SEDOL primary in UK. Single bond can have all 3 IDs simultaneously.

§ Why matter — settlement systems, brokerage records, trade reporting all use these IDs. Wrong ID = trade fails or settles wrong instrument. Always verify ID before large institutional trade.

Bond price quote.

§ Vignette
A 7-year corporate bond with par value of $1,000 and an annual coupon rate of 4.5% is quoted at 96.75. The price an investor would pay (excluding accrued interest) is closest to:
  1. A$967.50 — quoted price 96.75 means 96.75% of par; price = 96.75% × $1,000 = $967.50.
  2. B$96.75 — the bond price equals the quoted number expressed in dollars.
  3. C$1,012.08 — quoted price 96.75 represents the price after adjusting for the 4.5% coupon premium.
Fixed Income · 08.01 — Instrument Features
08.01 — Features 13 / 28
Hint · Question 01

Spot the convention.

§ Convention Bond prices quoted as % of par value. 100 = par; < 100 = discount; > 100 = premium.
  1. Hint 1 Quoted price 96.75 = 96.75% of par = 96.75/100. Investor pays this fraction × par.
  2. Hint 2 Calculation: 0.9675 × $1,000 = $967.50. Bond trading at discount (below par) — likely because coupon rate < market YTM.
  3. Hint 3 Đáp án B (interpret 96.75 as dollar price) common error — quotes are percentages, not dollar amounts. Đáp án C makes up adjustment that doesn't exist.

Một corporate bond 7 năm với par value $1,000 và coupon rate annual 4.5% được quote tại 96.75. Giá investor sẽ trả (chưa tính accrued interest) gần nhất với:
  1. A$967.50 — quoted price 96.75 nghĩa 96.75% of par; price = 96.75% × $1,000 = $967.50.
  2. B$96.75 — giá bond bằng quoted number biểu thị dạng dollar.
  3. C$1,012.08 — quoted price 96.75 đại diện giá sau khi adjust cho coupon premium 4.5%.

08.01 — Features 15 / 28
Answer · Question 01

Answer: A — $967.50.

✓ Correct — A
Bond price convention universal: quotes là percentage of par value. Quoted 96.75 = 96.75% of par. Calculation: 0.9675 × $1,000 = $967.50. Bond trading at discount (below par) — typically khi coupon rate < current market YTM. Investor will earn coupon (4.5% × par) plus capital appreciation as price moves toward par at maturity. Convention applies to all bond markets globally.
✗ Wrong — B
Sai cốt lõi — bond quote 96.75 KHÔNG nghĩa $96.75 actual price. Quote là percentage. $96.75 cho a $1,000 par bond = quote 9.675 — would be deep distressed price. Common beginner error.
✗ Wrong — C
Sai logic — không có "coupon premium adjustment" trong quoted price. Quoted price là CLEAN price (excludes accrued interest). Coupon rate determines coupon $ payments, không phải bond price. Đáp án invents adjustment that doesn't exist trong bond market convention.
Key takeaway
Bond price quoted as % of par. Convention: 100 = par; < 100 = discount; > 100 = premium. Always multiply quoted price (as decimal) × par value to get dollar price. Standard $1,000 par US corporate; $100 par European; some €1,000 par Eurobonds. Clean price (quoted) excludes accrued interest; dirty price (settlement) = clean + accrued.

Covenant classification.

§ Vignette
A bond indenture includes the following four provisions: (i) The issuer must maintain a debt-to-EBITDA ratio of no more than 4.0×. (ii) The issuer cannot incur additional senior debt above $500 million without bondholder consent. (iii) The issuer must file audited financial statements with the trustee within 90 days of fiscal year-end. (iv) The issuer cannot sell major operating assets representing more than 25% of consolidated assets without bondholder consent. Which provisions are negative (restrictive) covenants?
  1. A(i) and (iii) only — both relate to financial discipline that issuer must maintain.
  2. B(ii) and (iv) only — these restrict actions issuer must not take (incurring debt above limit; selling major assets) without bondholder consent.
  3. CAll four — any contractual obligation in an indenture is by definition a negative covenant.
Fixed Income · 08.01 — Instrument Features
08.01 — Features 17 / 28
Hint · Question 02

Spot must do vs must not.

§ Distinction Affirmative = must do (maintain ratio, file reports, insure assets). Negative = must not do (cannot issue more debt, cannot sell assets, cannot pay big dividends).
  1. Hint 1 (i) "Must maintain debt-to-EBITDA ≤ 4.0×" — affirmative obligation to maintain certain financial ratio. Issuer doing something positive.
  2. Hint 2 (ii) "Cannot incur additional senior debt above $500M" — restrictive; prevents action. Negative covenant.
  3. Hint 3 (iii) "Must file audited financials" — affirmative obligation. (iv) "Cannot sell major assets" — restrictive. Pattern: "must" → affirmative; "cannot" → negative. Negative covenants = (ii) + (iv).

Dịch đề.

§ Vignette
Một bond indenture có 4 provisions: (i) Issuer phải maintain debt-to-EBITDA ≤ 4.0×. (ii) Issuer không thể incur additional senior debt trên $500M nếu không có bondholder consent. (iii) Issuer phải file audited financial statements với trustee trong vòng 90 ngày sau fiscal year-end. (iv) Issuer không thể sell major operating assets chiếm hơn 25% consolidated assets nếu không có bondholder consent. Provisions nào là negative (restrictive) covenants?
  1. A(i) và (iii) — cả hai liên quan financial discipline issuer phải maintain.
  2. B(ii) và (iv) — restrict actions issuer phải không làm (incur debt trên limit; sell major assets) without bondholder consent.
  3. CCả 4 — bất kỳ obligation nào trong indenture đều là negative covenant theo definition.

08.01 — Features 19 / 28
Answer · Question 02

Answer: B — (ii) + (iv).

✓ Correct — B
Distinction clear: Affirmative covenants = "must do" (positive obligations issuer commits to perform). Negative covenants = "must not do" (restrictive actions issuer prevented from taking). (i) Maintain ≤ 4.0× ratio = affirmative (must keep ratio in compliance). (ii) Cannot incur additional debt = negative (restricts financing action). (iii) Must file financials = affirmative (positive obligation). (iv) Cannot sell major assets = negative (restricts disposition action). Negative covenants = (ii) + (iv). Negative covenants protect bondholders against management actions that increase risk after bond issued (asset substitution, leveraged dividend recap).
✗ Wrong — A
Sai phân loại — (i) và (iii) đều là affirmative ("must do" actions: maintain ratio, file reports), không negative. Confuse "financial discipline" với "restrictive" — discipline đến từ obligation to maintain, không phải prohibition.
✗ Wrong — C
Sai cốt lõi — không phải mọi covenant là negative. Covenants split rõ 2 category: affirmative (must do) vs negative (must not do). Indenture has both types intentionally để cover both obligation forms.
Key takeaway
Memorize: Affirmative = must do (maintain ratios, file reports, insure collateral, use proceeds as stated). Negative = must not do (limit additional debt, restrict dividends/buybacks, prevent asset sales, prevent mergers without consent). Investment grade bonds typically loose covenants (issuer strength reduces need); high yield typically extensive covenant package.

Bond classification by currency.

§ Vignette
Toyota Motor Corporation, a Japanese company, issues a USD-denominated bond in the United States, registered with the SEC and complying with US securities regulations. Separately, the same company issues a USD-denominated bond in London (outside the US), targeting international investors and not registered with the SEC. The first bond is most likely classified as a:
  1. AYankee bond (foreign issuer in US, USD-denominated, US-registered); the second is a Eurodollar bond (USD bond outside US jurisdiction).
  2. BSamurai bond (Japanese issuer); the second is a Yankee bond (issued by Toyota in foreign market).
  3. CBoth are Yankee bonds because Toyota is the same issuer and both bonds are USD-denominated.

Hint · Question 03

Spot the market.

§ Definitions Yankee = non-US issuer in US (USD, SEC). Samurai = non-Japan issuer in Japan (JPY). Eurodollar = USD bond outside US jurisdiction.
  1. Hint 1 First bond: foreign issuer (Toyota = Japanese) + US market + USD + SEC registered → Yankee bond.
  2. Hint 2 Second bond: USD-denominated + issued outside US (London) + not SEC registered → Eurodollar bond. "Euro" prefix = bond outside currency's home country, not necessarily Europe.
  3. Hint 3 Samurai = bond by foreign issuer in Japan denominated JPY — neither bond fits this definition. Toyota's home market = Japan, so Toyota issuing JPY at home wouldn't be Samurai (it would be domestic).

Dịch đề.

§ Vignette
Toyota Motor Corporation, công ty Nhật, issue 1 bond denominated USD tại United States, đăng ký với SEC và tuân thủ US securities regulations. Riêng biệt, cùng công ty issue 1 bond USD-denominated tại London (ngoài US), nhắm đến international investors và không đăng ký SEC. Bond đầu tiên nhiều khả năng được phân loại là:
  1. AYankee bond (foreign issuer trong US, USD-denominated, US-registered); bond thứ hai là Eurodollar bond (USD bond outside US jurisdiction).
  2. BSamurai bond (Japanese issuer); bond thứ hai là Yankee bond (issued by Toyota trong foreign market).
  3. CCả hai là Yankee bonds vì Toyota là cùng issuer và cả hai bonds đều USD-denominated.

08.01 — Features 23 / 28
Answer · Question 03

Answer: A — Yankee + Eurodollar.

✓ Correct — A
Bond classification by issuer-currency-jurisdiction: (1) Yankee bond = bond issued by foreign entity (Toyota = Japanese) in the US market, denominated in USD, registered with the SEC. Subject to US securities laws + disclosure requirements. (2) Eurodollar bond = USD-denominated bond issued outside the US jurisdiction (London here), not SEC-registered. "Euro" prefix doesn't mean Europe — it means bond denominated in a currency outside that currency's home country (Eurodollar = USD outside US; Euroyen = JPY outside Japan). Eurobonds avoid heavy SEC regulatory burden, attract international investors.
✗ Wrong — B
Sai phân loại Samurai — Samurai bond = foreign issuer in Japan denominated JPY. Toyota issuing in Japan wouldn't be Samurai (Toyota là home country issuer). Toyota issuing USD trong US là Yankee, không Samurai.
✗ Wrong — C
Sai cốt lõi — classification phụ thuộc jurisdiction of issuance, không chỉ issuer + currency. London-issued bond outside US = Eurobond regardless of who issues. Same Toyota có thể issue Yankee (in US) và Eurodollar (outside US) cùng lúc — đó là 2 different bonds với different regulatory regime.
Key takeaway
Memorize bond classifications: Domestic (issuer + currency same country); Yankee (foreign issuer in US, USD, SEC); Samurai (foreign issuer in Japan, JPY); Bulldog (foreign issuer in UK, GBP); Eurobond (denominated outside currency's home country, không SEC-registered, attractive for cross-border issuers seeking lighter regulation).
