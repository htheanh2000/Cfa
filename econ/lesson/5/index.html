Concept · definition

What is Geopolitics?

Geopolitics = interactions giữa states và non-state actors định hình global economy + financial markets. Không chỉ là politics — nó là politics × geography × economics.

  1. Politics shapes capital flows Trade policy, sanctions, FDI restrictions trực tiếp redirect dòng tiền, goods, và technology xuyên biên giới.
  2. Geography constrains choices Resource endowment (dầu, rare earths, semiconductors) + choke points (Strait of Hormuz, Taiwan Strait) quyết định bargaining power.
  3. Markets price the outcomes Equity, FX, commodity, bond markets phản ánh geopolitical risk real-time — thường trước cả policy announcement.

Actors · taxonomy

The 4 actor types.

CFA L1 yêu cầu phân biệt 4 loại actors. Mỗi loại có mandate, authority, và incentive khác nhau.

01
States
National governments — US, China, EU members. Monopoly on force trong lãnh thổ, quyết định chính sách tiền tệ + tài khóa + thương mại.
02
Multilaterals
Multi-country orgs không lãnh thổ: UN, WTO, IMF, World Bank, G20. Thiết lập rules, dispute resolution, financial assistance.
03
Supranational
Regional unions với shared sovereignty: EU, ASEAN, AU. Member states nhường một phần quyền (e.g. EU: monetary policy, trade).
04
Non-state actors
Corporations (MNCs), NGOs, terrorist groups, rating agencies. No territory, nhưng có thể shift markets (e.g. oil majors, S&P downgrade).

Trends · globalization

Globalization & slowbalization.

Globalization = integration của trade, capital, labor, technology xuyên biên giới. Peak ~2008, chậm lại từ đó — xu hướng gọi là slowbalization.

01
Trade openness
Tỷ lệ (Exports + Imports) / GDP. Tăng mạnh 1980–2008, phẳng/giảm từ 2008 do GFC + trade wars.
02
Capital flows
FDI + portfolio flows. Bùng nổ pre-2008, giảm post-GFC, fragmented theo geopolitical blocks (US vs China).
03
Migration
Labor movement. Tăng steady nhưng gặp backlash chính trị (Brexit, US immigration debates).
04
Tech transfer
Knowledge + IP diffusion. Ngày càng restricted (US chip export controls 2022+, China indigenous innovation push).

State behavior · two modes

Cooperation vs Confrontation.

Cooperation — positive-sum
Treaties
Trade agreements: NAFTA → USMCA, CPTPP, RCEP
Institutions
WTO (trade rules), IMF (loans), World Bank (development)
Alliances
NATO (defense), ASEAN (regional coordination)
Integration
Customs unions, common markets, monetary unions (EU/eurozone)
Confrontation — zero/negative-sum
Tariffs
Import taxes (US–China 2018+ trade war)
Quotas
Quantity caps trên imports
Sanctions
Trade bans, SWIFT exclusion (Russia 2022)
Export controls
Advanced chip restrictions → China (US, 2022)

§ Gradient — không phải binary. Hai nước có thể đồng thời cooperate (trade) và confront (tech sanctions) — gọi là "cooperation-rivalry" hoặc "frenemies". US–China là ví dụ kinh điển.

Cooperation · instruments

Economic integration ladder.

Cooperation có degrees. Mỗi bước sâu hơn yêu cầu nhường sovereignty nhiều hơn.

  1. Free Trade Area (FTA) Zero tariffs giữa members; mỗi nước giữ tariff riêng với ngoài. Ví dụ: USMCA.
  2. Customs Union FTA + common external tariff. Ví dụ: Mercosur, Southern African Customs Union.
  3. Common Market Customs union + free movement của labor + capital. Ví dụ: EU Single Market.
  4. Monetary / Economic Union Common market + shared currency (Eurozone) + coordinated fiscal policy. Mức integration cao nhất.

Confrontation · instruments

Tools of economic pressure.

TR
Tariffs
Import taxes. Tăng giá imports, bảo hộ domestic industry. Gây retaliation → trade war.
QT
Quotas
Giới hạn số lượng imports. Tạo scarcity rent; ít transparent hơn tariff.
SC
Sanctions
Trade bans, asset freezes, SWIFT exclusion. Targeted (individuals/entities) hoặc broad (country-level).
EC
Export controls
Chặn outbound công nghệ nhạy cảm (chips, dual-use tech). Khác tariff: không về giá mà về access.
FX
Currency manipulation
Intentional devaluation để boost exports. Cáo buộc khó prove; IMF / US Treasury monitor.
ML
Military action
Extreme escalation. Severe market impact — spike oil, flight to safety, supply-chain disruption.

Risk · taxonomy

Three types of geopolitical risk.

  1. Event risk — discrete, known calendar Điểm thời gian xác định: elections, referendums, treaty signings. Ví dụ: Brexit referendum (2016), US presidential election. Markets có thể hedge trước.
  2. Exogenous risk — sudden, unpredictable Shocks từ ngoài: invasion, terrorist attack, natural disaster. Ví dụ: Russia–Ukraine invasion (Feb 2022), 9/11. Khó hedge vì timing random.
  3. Thematic risk — slow-burn, structural Xu hướng dài hạn: trade fragmentation, decoupling, climate policy shifts. Ví dụ: US–China tech decoupling (2018+). Tác động qua nhiều năm.

Impact · 4 asset classes

How markets react.

Geopolitical shock ảnh hưởng khác nhau đến commodities, FX, equity, bonds. CFA L1 yêu cầu biết typical direction.

CM
Commodities
Oil, natural gas SPIKE khi Middle East / Russia tensions. Gold tăng (safe-haven). Agricultural commodities biến động theo export bans.
FX
Currencies
Safe-haven appreciate: USD, CHF (Swiss franc), JPY. EM currencies depreciate. Risk-off → capital flows vào DM.
EQ
Equities
Flight to quality — defensives (utilities, staples) outperform; cyclicals (tech, consumer discretionary) underperform. Defense stocks spike on conflict.
BD
Bonds
US Treasuries rally (yields fall) — flight to quality. Credit spreads widen, especially HY + EM. German Bunds, JGBs cũng benefit.

Case studies · recent

Three landmark events.

  1. US–China trade war (2018+) Thematic risk. Tariffs trên $370B imports, export controls lên chips, CFIUS blocking Chinese acquisitions. Impact: supply-chain reshoring, semiconductor stocks volatile, CNY weaken.
  2. Russia–Ukraine war (Feb 2022) Exogenous + event. Western sanctions, SWIFT exclusion, asset freeze. Energy shock: Brent +30%, European gas 10×. Ruble collapse then recover. Wheat +40%. EU pivot khỏi Russian gas.
  3. Brexit referendum (Jun 2016) Event risk, outcome surprise. GBP sụp −10% qua đêm (low 1.32). FTSE 100 phục hồi nhanh (weak GBP boosts exporters). Multi-year uncertainty → slow investment in UK.

Portfolio · implications

Managing geopolitical risk.

§ Portfolio playbook Geopolitical risk là non-diversifiable ở global level, nhưng có thể hedge và tilt portfolio để giảm drawdown trong stress scenarios.
  1. Geographic diversification Spread assets across regions với low correlation (US + EU + EM Asia + LatAm). Giảm concentration risk từ single-country event.
  2. Geopolitical hedges Long gold, USD, defense stocks, energy. Options (puts on EM equity, calls on VIX) để hedge tail risk.
  3. Watch home-country bias Investors over-allocate domestic — tăng exposure đến local political shocks. CFA khuyến nghị global benchmark thay vì home-heavy.
Key takeaway
Geopolitical risk không thể eliminate — chỉ có thể diversify away idiosyncratic phần và hedge systemic phần. Position sizing + scenario analysis là primary tools.

Recap · before practice

Four things to remember.

01 — Four actor types. States (national govts), Multilaterals (UN, WTO, IMF), Supranational (EU, ASEAN), Non-state (MNCs, NGOs, terrorist groups). Supranational khác Multilateral: supranational có shared sovereignty over territory.

02 — Two behavior modes. Cooperation (treaties, alliances, economic integration) vs Confrontation (tariffs, quotas, sanctions, export controls). Có thể xảy ra đồng thời giữa cùng 2 states.

03 — Three risk types. Event (known date), Exogenous (surprise shock), Thematic (slow-burn trend). Time horizon và hedgeability khác nhau.

04 — Market reaction pattern. Risk-off: commodities (oil, gold) UP, safe-haven FX (USD/CHF/JPY) UP, defensives UP, Treasuries UP, EM + cyclicals + credit spreads WIDER.

Classify the actor.

§ Vignette
The European Union negotiates trade agreements on behalf of its 27 member states, enforces a common external tariff, and operates a shared currency (the euro) across most members. Individual member states have surrendered significant policy authority in exchange for membership. Which classification best describes the EU?
  1. AMultilateral organization — like the WTO or IMF.
  2. BSupranational organization — involves shared sovereignty among member states.
  3. CNon-state actor — because it is not itself a nation.

CFA Level I ECON · 03.05 — Q1 VIỆT
Econ · 03.05 19 / 28
Answer · Question 01

Answer: B — Supranational.

✓ Correct — B
EU là supranational vì có shared sovereignty: members nhường quyền cho common institutions (European Commission, ECB, European Parliament). ECB set monetary policy cho eurozone, common external tariff là customs union feature. Đây là binding authority đè lên member states.
✗ Wrong — A
Multilateral (WTO, IMF, UN, G20) là forums để states coordinate — KHÔNG có territory và KHÔNG có shared sovereignty. Members có thể exit bất cứ lúc nào mà không cần đàm phán với union. EU khác: Brexit mất nhiều năm đàm phán vì có binding integration.
✗ Wrong — C
Non-state actors = corporations (Apple, ExxonMobil), NGOs (Red Cross), terrorist groups. EU là government union, composed of states, không phải non-state.
Key takeaway
Test phân biệt: "Có shared sovereignty không?" Có → supranational (EU, eurozone parts, ASEAN partially). Không, chỉ là forum → multilateral (WTO, IMF). Không phải government at all → non-state.

Identify the tool.

§ Vignette
In response to rising tensions, Country A implements a policy that restricts the outbound shipment of advanced semiconductor manufacturing equipment to firms in Country B. The measure does not raise prices on goods entering Country A and does not limit imports; it specifically blocks the transfer of certain sensitive technologies abroad. This tool is best described as:
  1. AA tariff — an import tax levied at the border.
  2. BAn export control — a restriction on outbound transfer of sensitive goods or technology.
  3. CA form of economic integration — cooperative in nature.
CFA Level I ECON · 03.05 — Q2 ORIGINAL
Econ · 03.05 21 / 28
Hint · Question 02

Spot the keywords.

§ Keywords Restricts outbound shipment, advanced semiconductor equipment, does not raise prices on imports, blocks transfer of sensitive tech abroad.
  1. Hint 1 "Outbound" = going out, tức export. Tariff là tax trên import (inbound). Loại A ngay.
  2. Hint 2 "Does not raise prices" = không phải về giá. Tariff về giá, quota về số lượng import. Đây là về access — ai được mua cái gì. Đặc trưng của export control.
  3. Hint 3 "Sensitive technologies" là classic language của export control (dual-use tech, advanced chips). Ví dụ thực: US chip export controls lên China 2022.

Dịch đề.

§ Vignette
Trước căng thẳng tăng cao, Country A ban hành chính sách hạn chế việc xuất khẩu thiết bị sản xuất semiconductor tiên tiến cho các firms ở Country B. Biện pháp này không tăng giá hàng hóa nhập vào Country A và không giới hạn imports; nó chặn việc chuyển giao một số sensitive technologies ra nước ngoài. Tool này chính xác nhất là:
  1. ATariff — thuế import đánh tại biên giới.
  2. BExport control — hạn chế xuất ra nước ngoài các hàng/tech nhạy cảm.
  3. CMột hình thức economic integration — mang tính cooperative.
CFA Level I ECON · 03.05 — Q2 VIỆT
Econ · 03.05 23 / 28
Answer · Question 02

Answer: B — Export control.

✓ Correct — B
Export control hạn chế outbound flow của sensitive goods/technology. Không về giá, không về quantity của imports — về access: ai được phép mua. Đây là confrontation tool phổ biến trong tech rivalry (US chip restrictions lên China 2022+, CHIPS Act).
✗ Wrong — A
Tariff là tax trên imports vào Country A, làm tăng giá hàng nhập. Vignette nói rõ "does not raise prices" và focus là "outbound" — hoàn toàn ngược với tariff.
✗ Wrong — C
Economic integration (FTA, customs union, common market) là cooperation — mở rộng thương mại, không restrict. Vignette là confrontation (tensions, restrictions). Hoàn toàn ngược direction.
Key takeaway
Direction test: inbound restriction trên giá → tariff. Inbound restriction trên số lượng → quota. Outbound restriction trên tech/goods → export control. Financial/broad bans → sanctions.

Predict market reaction.

§ Vignette
A major conflict erupts in a key oil-producing region, disrupting global energy supply. Western nations impose broad sanctions on the aggressor state, including SWIFT exclusion and central-bank asset freezes. Which combination of short-term market reactions is most likely?
  1. AOil prices fall, US Treasury yields rise, USD depreciates, defense stocks decline.
  2. BOil prices spike, US Treasury yields fall (flight to quality), USD and CHF appreciate, defense stocks rally.
  3. COil prices spike, EM currencies appreciate, credit spreads narrow, cyclicals outperform defensives.

Hint · Question 03

Spot the keywords.

§ Keywords Oil-producing region conflict, supply disruption, broad sanctions, SWIFT exclusion, asset freeze. Kinh điển exogenous risk + commodity shock.
  1. Hint 1 Supply disruption ở oil region → oil prices spike (không giảm). Loại A ngay.

CFA Level I ECON · 03.05 — Q3 VIỆT
Econ · 03.05 27 / 28
Answer · Question 03

Answer: B — Classic risk-off.

✓ Correct — B
Textbook risk-off response: (1) Oil spikes do supply shock; (2) UST yields fall (bond prices up) khi investors flight to quality — Treasuries + Bunds + JGBs; (3) USD + CHF + JPY appreciate do safe-haven demand; (4) Defense stocks rally do expected increase in military spending. Đây khớp với Russia–Ukraine 2022 pattern.
✗ Wrong — A
Sai hầu như mọi direction: oil DROP khi có disruption là ngược lý thuyết supply–demand. UST yields tăng = bond prices giảm, ngược với flight to quality. USD depreciate ngược với safe-haven behavior. Defense stocks giảm khi có conflict cũng sai.
✗ Wrong — C
Oil spike đúng, nhưng EM currencies depreciate trong risk-off (capital flows khỏi EM), không appreciate. Credit spreads widen (not narrow) khi risk aversion tăng. Cyclicals underperform defensives, không outperform.
Key takeaway
Risk-off mnemonic: "Up — Down — Up — Up". UP: commodities (supply-affected) + safe-haven FX (USD/CHF/JPY) + defensive sectors + bond prices (yields down). DOWN: EM FX + cyclicals + credit spread tighteners. Học pattern này một lần, apply cho mọi geopolitical shock question.
