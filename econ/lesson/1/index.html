Concept · Block A

Law of Demand.

Khi P cao → Q thấp. Underlying 2 effects + 2 exceptions.

  1. Substitution effectGiá A tăng relative to B → consumer switch sang B. Strength phụ thuộc availability of substitutes. Insulin không có substitute → substitution ≈ 0.
  2. Income effectGiá tăng → real income giảm → consumer cut consumption toàn bộ. Mạnh cho hàng % lớn của budget (housing, food).
  3. Exceptions — Giffen & VeblenGiffen: staple food cho nghèo, income effect DOMINATES → P↑ thì Q↑. Veblen: luxury status, high P = prestige → Q↑.
CFA Level IECON · 03.01 — LAW OF DEMAND
Econ · 03.0104 / 50
Demand · formula

The demand function.

§ Linear form
Qd = a − b · P
a = intercept (max demand at P=0). b = |slope| > 0. Inverse: P = (a − Q)/b.
§ General form — 7 variables
Qd = f(P, I, Ps, Pc, T, E, N)
I income · Ps substitute · Pc complement · T tastes · E expectations · N # buyers.

§ Movement vs Shift — P only → movement along. Other variable → shift.

Determinants

What makes demand elastic?

5 yếu tố quyết định PED của một good. Exam trap hay test student về direction của each factor.

01
Substitutes availability
Nhiều substitute gần gũi → elastic. Insulin (no subst) |PED|≈0.1; Coca-Cola (Pepsi, Dr Pepper) |PED|≈3.
02
Share of income
Hàng chiếm % lớn của budget → elastic (housing, cars). Salt (<0.1% budget) → inelastic.
03
Necessity vs luxury
Necessity (medicine, food) → inelastic. Luxury (vacation, high-end) → elastic.
04
Time horizon
Longer time → more elastic (consumers adjust habits, find substitutes). SR oil PED ≈ −0.2, LR ≈ −0.6.
05
Market definition
Narrow market → elastic ("bánh mì brand X"). Broad → inelastic ("bánh mì overall"). Exam trap về scope.
§
Combined effect
Factors tương tác — narrow luxury market với nhiều subst = very elastic (iPhone Pro max |PED|≈5).

Concept + formula

Law of Supply.

§ Linear supply
Qs = c + d · P
c thường âm (intercept). d > 0 reflect law of supply. Slope magnitude = supply elasticity proxy.
  1. 5 supply shifters(1) Input costs ↓ → supply right. (2) Technology ↑ → supply right. (3) Taxes/regulation ↑ → left. (4) # producers ↑ → right. (5) Price expectations ↑ → withhold → left.
  2. Short-run vs Long-runSR: capacity fixed, entry/exit limited → inelastic. LR: firms adjust → elastic.
CFA Level IECON · 03.01 — SUPPLY
Econ · 03.0107 / 50
Determinants

What makes supply flexible?

01
Time horizon
SR: fixed capacity → inelastic. LR: entry/exit + scale capacity → elastic. Main factor for PES.
02
Input availability
Easy-access inputs (commodity labor, raw materials) → elastic. Specialized inputs (rare earth, expert) → inelastic.
03
Storage capability
Non-perishable goods (grain, metals) → elastic (can stock up/release). Perishables (fresh fish, flowers) → inelastic.
04
Spare capacity
Underutilized plants/firms → elastic. Running at 100% capacity → inelastic.

§ Example — agricultural supply highly inelastic SR (crop already planted), elastic LR (next season adjust planting). Semiconductor supply even more inelastic SR (fabs take 2-3 years to build).

Edge cases

Perfectly elastic vs inelastic.

Perfectly elastic |PED| = ∞
Shape
Horizontal demand curve
Behavior
Any P change → Q jumps to 0 hoặc ∞
Example
Commodities in perfect competition (wheat, gold — firm price-taker)
Interpretation
Consumer có perfect substitute → không chịu $0.01 tăng
Perfectly inelastic |PED| = 0
Shape
Vertical demand curve
Behavior
Q giữ nguyên regardless of P
Example
Insulin (life-saving), addictive drugs, urgent medical care
Interpretation
Consumer không có alternative → buy at any price

§ Unit-elastic (|PED|=1): rectangular hyperbola. TR constant regardless of P. Rare trong thực tế nhưng theoretical benchmark.

Shift dynamics

When demand shifts.

D →
Demand shifts RIGHT
Causes: Income ↑ (normal good), tastes favorable, subst price ↑, complement price ↓. Result: P*↑, Q*↑.
D ←
Demand shifts LEFT
Causes: Income ↓ (recession), tastes unfavorable, subst price ↓. Result: P*↓, Q*↓.
§
Magnitude rule
Steeper (inelastic) supply → price bears more. Flatter (elastic) supply → quantity bears more.
§
Real example
COVID: demand for masks shifted right dramatically → P spike (SR inelastic supply). LR: supply caught up, P fell.

CFA Level IECON · 03.01 — S SHIFT
Econ · 03.0113 / 50
Dual shifts

When both shift.

Khi D và S cả hai shift cùng lúc: luôn có 1 biến certain và 1 biến ambiguous. Exam favorite trap.

01
D right + S right
Q↑ certain (both push up). P ambiguous (D pushes up, S pushes down).
02
D left + S left
Q↓ certain. P ambiguous.
03
D right + S left
P↑ certain (both push up). Q ambiguous.
04
D left + S right
P↓ certain. Q ambiguous.

§ Cheat sheet — Same direction: quantity certain, price ambiguous. Opposite directions: price certain, quantity ambiguous. Magnitude of each shift determines ambiguous variable.

Block C · Elasticity

How sensitive is quantity?

3 loại elasticity, tất cả unit-free (% ratio), comparable across markets.

PED
Price Elasticity of Demand
%ΔQd/%ΔP. Luôn âm. |PED|>1 elastic, <1 inelastic, =1 unit-elastic.
IED
Income Elasticity
%ΔQ/%ΔI. >0 normal, <0 inferior. >1 luxury, <1 necessity.
CPE
Cross-Price Elasticity
%ΔQA/%ΔPB. >0 substitutes, <0 complements, ≈0 unrelated.
§
PED varies along linear curve
Trên cùng linear demand, PED lớn ở phần trên (high P, low Q), nhỏ ở phần dưới. Không phải constant.
CFA Level IECON · 03.01 — ELASTICITY CONCEPT
Econ · 03.0115 / 50
Elasticity · formulas

The equations.

§ PED — 2 methods
Simple: PED = (ΔQ/Q1) / (ΔP/P1)
Midpoint/arc: PED = [ΔQ/avg(Q)] / [ΔP/avg(P)]
Midpoint accurate hơn cho large changes. Exam dùng cả 2 — đọc kỹ.
§ IED & CPE
IED = (%ΔQ)/(%ΔI) · CPEA,B = (%ΔQA)/(%ΔPB)

Worked example.

CFA Level IECON · 03.01 — TR TEST
Econ · 03.0118 / 50
TR test · example

2 products — opposite outcomes.

§ ScenarioCùng raise price 10%. Product A (PED=−2.0): Q giảm 20%. Product B (PED=−0.5): Q giảm 5%. Starting P=$10, Q=1000 cả hai. So sánh TR.
§ Product A — Elastic
TR0 = 10×1000 = $10,000. TR1 = 11×800 = $8,800
ΔTR = −$1,200 → elastic + P↑ → TR↓ ✓
§ Product B — Inelastic
TR0 = $10,000. TR1 = 11×950 = $10,450
ΔTR = +$450 → inelastic + P↑ → TR↑ ✓

§ Insight — Same 10% price hike, opposite outcomes based on elasticity. Firm pricing strategy phải match demand elasticity.

Welfare economics

Consumer & Producer Surplus.

CS
Consumer Surplus
Willingness to pay − actual price. Triangle dưới demand, trên P*.
PS
Producer Surplus
Price received − marginal cost. Triangle trên supply, dưới P*.
TS
Total = CS + PS
Max tại competitive equilibrium. Any intervention reduces TS.
DWL
Deadweight Loss
Lost TS from market distortion. Measures efficiency cost.

§ 5 sources of DWL — tax wedge · price ceiling (shortage) · price floor (surplus) · monopoly (restrict Q) · trade barriers.

CFA Level IECON · 03.01 — SURPLUS
Econ · 03.0120 / 50
Surplus · example

Calculate CS + PS.

§ ScenarioDemand: P = 100 − Q. Supply: P = 20 + Q. Tính equilibrium, CS, PS, TS.
§ Step 1 — Equilibrium
Set equal: 100 − Q = 20 + Q → Q* = 40, P* = 60
§ Step 2 — CS & PS (triangle areas)
CS = ½ × 40 × (100 − 60) = $800
PS = ½ × 40 × (60 − 20) = $800
TS = CS + PS = $1,600

§ Geometric: CS triangle có base = Q* (40) và height = (max willingness to pay − P*). Tương tự PS.

Tax incidence

Who bears the tax?

Burden KHÔNG phụ thuộc who government charges — mà phụ thuộc elasticity tương đối. Inelastic side bears more.

§ Tax incidence formula
Consumer burden = PES / (PES + |PED|)
Producer burden = |PED| / (PES + |PED|)
Cộng 2 shares = 1. Inelastic side absorbs more burden.
  1. Inelastic D + elastic S → consumer bears mostGas, cigarettes, insulin — consumer can't avoid tax via quitting. 70-90% on consumer.
  2. Elastic D + inelastic S → producer bears mostFresh produce, competitive commoditized goods. Producer absorbs để maintain sales.
CFA Level IECON · 03.01 — TAX INCIDENCE
Econ · 03.0122 / 50
Tax · example

$10 tax split.

§ ScenarioGovernment imposes $10/unit tax. PED = −0.5, PES = +2.0. Initial P = $50, Q = 1,000. Compute burden split + post-tax prices.
§ Step 1 — Burden shares
Consumer = 2.0 / (2.0 + 0.5) = 80% → $8 of the $10
Producer = 0.5 / 2.5 = 20% → $2 of the $10
§ Step 2 — Post-tax prices
Consumer pays: $50 + $8 = $58
Producer receives: $50 − $2 = $48
Wedge = $10 = tax ✓

§ Q drops from 1000 → ~900 (price hike dampens demand). Tax revenue = $10 × 900 = $9,000. DWL = ½ × $10 × 100 = $500.

Government intervention

Price Ceiling — rent control.

Legal max price below P*. Intent: protect consumers. Effect: persistent shortage + welfare loss.

§ ScenarioMarket rent P* = $1,000. Government sets ceiling $700 (30% below market).
  1. Direct effect — shortageAt $700: Qd > Qs. More tenants want apartments than landlords willing to supply.
  2. Indirect effects — quality, black marketLandlords reduce maintenance (lower quality to match lower P). Waiting lists. Illegal key fees. Under-the-table payments.
  3. Who benefits vs losesExisting tenants (before ceiling): benefit. New tenants: can't find housing. Landlords: lose PS. Market: DWL + reduced supply LR (fewer new buildings).
CFA Level IECON · 03.01 — CEILING
Econ · 03.0124 / 50
Government intervention

Price Floor — minimum wage.

Legal min price above P*. Intent: protect workers/producers. Effect: persistent surplus + unemployment.

§ ScenarioMarket wage P* = $12/hour. Minimum wage $15 (25% above market).
  1. Direct effect — surplus of laborAt $15: Qs (workers wanting to work) > Qd (firms hiring). Surplus = unemployment.
  2. Who benefits vs losesRetained workers: benefit (higher wage). Marginal workers: priced out, unemployed. Firms: higher cost → automate, reduce hours, raise prices.
  3. Elasticity mattersIf labor demand inelastic (skilled workers): small unemployment, big wage gain. If elastic (unskilled, fast food): large unemployment.
CFA Level IECON · 03.01 — FLOOR
Econ · 03.0125 / 50
Government intervention

Subsidy — mirror of tax.

§ Subsidy benefit formula (mirror of tax incidence)
Consumer benefit = PES / (PES + |PED|)
Same logic: inelastic side benefits more. If demand inelastic, consumers get most subsidy via lower prices.
  1. EV subsidies — who benefits?If EV demand elastic (many alternatives), producers (Tesla, GM) benefit more. If inelastic (eco-conscious buyers), consumers benefit via lower prices.
  2. Agricultural subsidiesFood demand inelastic → consumers benefit via lower food prices. Common policy but creates overproduction (surplus), DWL.
  3. Housing subsidies (affordable housing)Housing demand inelastic → renters benefit. But subsidy → demand curve shift right → price may rise overall, partial offsetting intended effect.

Econ · 03.0127 / 50
Block E · Consumer theory

Utility & Diminishing MU.

Consumer theory giải thích microfoundation của law of demand. Utility = satisfaction từ consumption.

§ Total Utility & Marginal Utility
TU(Q) = tổng satisfaction tại level Q
MU(Q) = ΔTU/ΔQ = utility từ 1 unit thêm
Law of Diminishing MU: mỗi additional unit provides less MU than previous. TU still rises but at decreasing rate.
  1. Pizza exampleSlice 1 MU = 10 (hungry, delicious). Slice 2 MU = 7. Slice 3 MU = 3. Slice 4 MU = 0 (full). Slice 5 MU < 0 (sick). TU maxes out where MU = 0.
  2. Links to demand curveConsumer buys thêm chỉ khi MU ≥ price. Diminishing MU → demand curve dốc xuống.
CFA Level IECON · 03.01 — UTILITY
Econ · 03.0128 / 50
Consumer theory

Indifference + Budget.

§ Indifference curve
Các combinations của (X, Y) cho cùng utility U
Slope = −MUX/MUY = −MRS
MRS (Marginal Rate of Substitution) = lượng Y consumer willing to give up for 1 more X. Diminishing MRS → curve convex to origin.
§ Budget constraint
PX · X + PY · Y = I
Slope = −PX/PY = relative price ratio
Line representing all affordable bundles given income I.
CFA Level IECON · 03.01 — INDIFFERENCE
Econ · 03.0129 / 50
Consumer theory

Consumer equilibrium.

§ Equal marginal utility per dollar rule
MUX / PX = MUY / PY = ... = MUN / PN
Consumer maximizes utility khi MU per dollar spent bằng nhau cross all goods. Else: reallocate from low-MU/$ good to high-MU/$ good.
  1. Geometric interpretationOptimal bundle tại điểm indifference curve tangent to budget line. MRS = price ratio tại đây.
  2. Adjustment processNếu MUX/PX > MUY/PY: consumer buy thêm X, less Y. MUX giảm (diminishing), MUY tăng → equilibrium restored.
  3. Effect of price changePX ↑ → MUX/PX ↓ → consumer buy less X. Đây là microfoundation của law of demand.
CFA Level IECON · 03.01 — EQUILIBRIUM
Econ · 03.0130 / 50
Consumer theory

Income vs Substitution effect.

Khi P thay đổi, total effect decomposes thành 2 parts — understanding này explain Giffen/inferior goods.

SE
Substitution effect
Same utility level, new relative prices. Consumer switches TOWARD cheaper good. Always negative (P↑ → Q↓).
IE
Income effect
Purchasing power change từ price move. Sign depends on good type.
Normal
Total effect
SE (−) + IE (−) = total (−). P↑ → Q↓↓ (reinforcing). Standard law of demand.
Inferior
Partial offset
SE (−) + IE (+) = total usually (−) but smaller. P↑ → Q↓ still, but less than normal.
Giffen
Reversal
IE (+) DOMINATES SE (−) → total (+). P↑ → Q↑. Violates law of demand. Extreme inferior goods in poverty.
§
Exam trap
Student thường confuse Giffen (income effect dominates) với Veblen (status effect dominates). Khác nhau về mechanism.

Demand curve anomalies

Demand that doesn't slope down.

01
Giffen goods
Inferior staple + IE dominates. Irish potato famine 1845 (historical evidence). Rare modern.
02
Veblen goods
Status/luxury. High P signals prestige → demand rises. Hermès, Rolex. "Conspicuous consumption" (Thorstein Veblen 1899).
03
Network effects
Value increases with # users. Facebook, Uber, crypto. Early stage: P rise attracts buyers (hype + FOMO).
04
Speculative demand
Asset bubbles: P↑ → more buying expected P will rise more. Bitcoin, housing 2006. Unsustainable but real.

§ Investment implication — Identifying upward-sloping demand segments helps investors (network effect stocks, luxury, commodities in shortage). But speculative bubbles eventually pop.

Answer: B — Elastic, TR ↓ $3,200.

✓ Correct — B
