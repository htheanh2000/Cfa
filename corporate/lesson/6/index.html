Business model · concept

The four W's.

Business model mô tả cách firm create, deliver, và capture value. Nó trả lời 4 câu hỏi lõi: ai là customer, sản phẩm gì, kênh nào, kiếm tiền ra sao.

§ Core definition (CFA Institute)
Business Model = Who + What + Where + How
Who — target customer (B2C, B2B, B2G). What — value proposition + product/service. Where — channels + geographic markets. How — revenue model, pricing, cost structure, key resources.

§ Khác biệt với strategy — Business model là "how it works"; strategy là "how we win" vs đối thủ. Business model có thể identical giữa nhiều firm; strategy thì unique.

Business model · 5 building blocks

The five components.

01
Customer
Target segment: B2C (mass / niche), B2B (enterprise / SMB), B2G. Define bằng demographics, needs, willingness to pay.
02
Value Proposition
Lý do customer chọn firm này. Có thể là price, performance, convenience, brand, customization, status.
03
Channels
Cách firm reach customer: direct (own store, website), indirect (retailer, distributor), digital, omnichannel.
04
Revenue Model
Cách firm thu tiền: one-time sale, subscription, freemium, advertising, licensing, marketplace fee.
05
Cost Structure
Fixed-heavy (airlines, telecom) vs variable-heavy (consulting). Drives operating leverage và scalability.
06
Key Resources
Brand, IP, network effects, data, supply chain, talent. Đây là moat — defensibility lâu dài.

Revenue · 6 archetypes

How firms make money.

01
Transactional
One-time sale per unit. Retail, manufacturing, e-commerce. Revenue lumpy, đo bằng GMV + AOV.
02
Subscription
Recurring fee (monthly/annual). SaaS, streaming, telecom. Predictable MRR/ARR — analyst yêu thích.
03
Freemium
Free tier để acquire, premium tier để monetize. Spotify, Dropbox. Conversion 2-5% là healthy.
04
Advertising
User free, advertiser pays. Google, Meta, TV. Đo bằng CPM, CPC, ARPU. Vulnerable to ad cycle.
05
Licensing / Royalty
Cấp phép IP, brand, technology. Pharma, software, franchising (McDonald's). High margin, low capex.
06
Marketplace / Commission
Take rate trên GMV (Uber 25%, Airbnb 15%). Asset-light, scale rất nhanh nhờ network effects.

Freemium · advertising · licensing · marketplace

Beyond the basics.

  1. Freemium — convert free users Spotify (~46% premium conversion), Dropbox (~4%), LinkedIn. Cost cho free tier phải đủ thấp để serve "for free" sustainable. KPI: conversion rate, payback period.
  2. Advertising — eyeballs là sản phẩm Google ($300B+ revenue), Meta. User free → firm bán attention cho advertiser. Đo: CPM, CPC, ARPU. Risk: ad cycle, regulation (privacy laws như GDPR).
  3. Licensing / Royalty — IP-heavy Pharma (patent royalties), Disney (character licensing), ARM (chip designs). Margins 70-90%, capex thấp. Risk: patent expiry, IP disputes.
  4. Marketplace — asset-light scale Uber (take rate ~25%), Airbnb (~15%), Etsy (~6.5%). Firm không own inventory hay supply. Network effects = winner-take-most dynamic. Bigger marketplace → more attractive cho cả 2 sides.

Pricing · 6 strategies

How firms set prices.

01
Cost-plus
Price = Cost + markup %. Đơn giản, dùng trong utilities, government contracts. Bỏ qua customer willingness-to-pay.
02
Value-based
Price = perceived value cho customer. Pharma, luxury, enterprise SaaS. Highest margin nếu execute tốt.
03
Dynamic / Surge
Price thay đổi realtime theo demand. Uber surge, airline yield mgmt, Amazon. Cần data + algorithm.
04
Penetration
Price thấp ban đầu để chiếm market share. Streaming wars (Disney+ $7), consumer apps freemium. Risk: hard to raise sau.
05
Skimming
Price cao ban đầu cho early adopter, giảm dần. Apple iPhone launch, Tesla, new pharma. Capture surplus theo segment.
06
Freemium pricing
$0 / tier, premium tier. Acquisition tool. Effective khi marginal cost gần 0 (digital products).

Cost · fixed vs variable

Fixed-heavy vs Variable-heavy.

Cost mix quyết định operating leverage — mức độ profit nhạy với revenue. Fixed-heavy = high leverage = boom-or-bust.

Fixed-heavy
Profile
Capex/R&D upfront, marginal cost thấp
Leverage
High operating leverage · profit volatile
Examples
Airlines · Telecom · Software · Streaming
Risk
Recession amplify losses · breakeven cao
Reward
Scale economies → margin expansion mạnh
Variable-heavy
Profile
Cost scale với volume, low fixed base
Leverage
Low operating leverage · margin stable
Examples
Consulting · Restaurants · Retail · Logistics
Risk
Margin compression khi input cost tăng
Reward
Resilient trong downturns · easier to flex

§ Scale economies — Fixed-heavy firms hưởng lợi nhiều từ scale (Netflix: 1 phim phục vụ 200M user, marginal cost ≈ 0). Variable-heavy ít leverage nhưng resilient.

Network effects · 2-sided platforms

When scale compounds.

§ Network effect rule Một firm có network effect khi giá trị của sản phẩm tăng theo số user. Hai loại chính: direct (Facebook — user nhiều → mỗi user thấy product hữu ích hơn) và indirect / 2-sided (Uber — driver nhiều → rider chờ ngắn → rider nhiều → driver kiếm nhiều → driver nhiều thêm).
§ Metcalfe's Law (intuition)
Network value ∝ n²
Value của network grows quadratic theo số node (n). Đây là why marketplace winners thường take most: Uber, Airbnb, Visa, Mastercard. Cold start problem: side nào cần seed trước? Uber subsidize driver bonuses; Airbnb stage host listings.

§ Examples — Uber (riders ↔ drivers) · Airbnb (guests ↔ hosts) · Visa (cardholders ↔ merchants) · App Store (users ↔ developers). Winner-take-most dynamic — second-place thường struggle.

KPIs · by business model

What analysts watch.

  1. Subscription / SaaS — MRR, ARR, churn, NDR, CAC payback Net Dollar Retention > 110% = expansion-led growth. Churn < 5%/year cho enterprise. CAC payback < 18 months = healthy unit economics.
  2. Marketplace — GMV, take rate, supply & demand growth GMV (Gross Merchandise Volume) là top-of-funnel; take rate × GMV = revenue. Track 2 sides riêng (drivers + riders, hosts + guests).
  3. Advertising — DAU/MAU, ARPU, CPM, time spent Engagement = leading indicator của ad inventory. Meta: DAU/MAU ratio ~78% = sticky. ARPU growth show monetization improving.
  4. Retail / Transactional — same-store sales, AOV, gross margin Comparable-store sales (SSS) loại bỏ new-store effect. Inventory turnover + gross margin trend cho thấy pricing power.

§ Universal — Across all models: gross margin trend, operating leverage, cash conversion, FCF margin. KPIs phải match business model — apply SaaS metrics cho retailer = nonsense.

Porter · 3 generic strategies

How firms compete.

  1. Cost leadership — be cheapest at scale Walmart, Costco, Ryanair, Vanguard. Win bằng efficiency, scale, supply chain mastery. Margin per unit thấp nhưng volume cao. Risk: race-to-bottom.
  2. Differentiation — unique value, premium price Apple, BMW, Hermès, Tesla. Win bằng brand, design, performance, ecosystem. Higher margin, less price-sensitive customer. Risk: commoditization.
  3. Focus — narrow segment, deep specialization Ferrari (super-luxury), Bloomberg Terminal (finance pros), Rolex. Cost focus or differentiation focus. Defensible niche, limited TAM.
§ When pricing power exists Firm có pricing power khi: (1) brand strong, (2) switching cost cao, (3) network effect, (4) IP/patent moat, (5) low elasticity (essential good). Test: firm raise price 5% — bao nhiêu % volume mất? <5% = strong pricing power.

Evolution · pivots that worked

When models change.

Business model không tĩnh. Firm pivot khi: (1) market shift, (2) tech disruption, (3) margin compression, (4) growth ceiling. Successful pivot reset valuation multiple.

  1. Adobe (2013) — perpetual license → Creative Cloud subscription Pre-pivot: lumpy revenue, piracy issue, P/E ~17×. Post-pivot: ARR predictable, P/E ~50×. Stock 10× trong 7 năm. Textbook subscription pivot.
  2. Netflix — DVD-by-mail → streaming → original content 3 mô hình kế tiếp. 2007 streaming launch. 2013 House of Cards = original content. Mỗi pivot expand TAM + raise barrier-to-entry.
  3. Marriott — own hotels → franchising / management contracts Asset-heavy → asset-light. Capture brand premium + reduce capex. ROE từ ~10% lên ~30%+. Hilton, IHG cùng pivot.
  4. Apple — hardware → Services (App Store, iCloud, Music) Services 2024 ~$96B revenue, margin ~70% vs hardware ~37%. Reduce dependence on iPhone unit cycle. Multiple expansion từ "hardware co" → "platform co".

Identify the model.

§ Vignette
A firm operates a digital platform connecting independent drivers with riders. The firm does not own any vehicles. It charges drivers a 25% commission on each completed trip and uses real-time algorithmic pricing during peak demand. Which combination of business-model attributes best describes the firm?
  1. ASubscription revenue · asset-heavy · cost leadership.
  2. BMarketplace / commission revenue · asset-light · two-sided network effects with dynamic pricing.
  3. CAdvertising revenue · variable-heavy · differentiation strategy.

Hint · Question 01

Spot the keywords.

§ Keywords Drivers ↔ riders = two-sided. Does not own vehicles = asset-light. 25% commission = take-rate / marketplace revenue. Real-time algorithmic pricing = dynamic.
  1. Hint 1 "Connecting drivers with riders" + take rate = textbook marketplace / 2-sided platform. Không phải subscription (no recurring fee from end-user).
  2. Hint 2 "Does not own vehicles" → asset-light. Khác hẳn Hertz / car rental (asset-heavy own fleet).
  3. Hint 3 Real-time pricing during peak = dynamic / surge pricing. Network effects vì nhiều driver → ít chờ → nhiều rider → nhiều driver.

§ Vignette
Một firm vận hành nền tảng kỹ thuật số kết nối tài xế độc lập với hành khách. Firm không sở hữu phương tiện nào. Firm thu của tài xế commission 25% trên mỗi chuyến hoàn thành và dùng thuật toán định giá thời gian thực vào giờ cao điểm. Tổ hợp đặc điểm business model nào mô tả tốt nhất firm này?
  1. ASubscription revenue · asset-heavy · cost leadership.
  2. BMarketplace / commission revenue · asset-light · 2-sided network effects với dynamic pricing.
  3. CAdvertising revenue · variable-heavy · differentiation strategy.
CFA Level I Corporate · 05.06 — Business Models

Answer · Question 01

Answer: B.

✓ Correct — B
Đây là Uber-archetype. Marketplace/commission revenue (25% take rate trên GMV). Asset-light (không own vehicle). Two-sided network effect (driver ↔ rider value loop). Dynamic pricing (algorithmic surge). Tất cả 4 attributes match exactly.
✗ Wrong — A
Subscription = recurring flat fee (Netflix, Spotify). Firm trong vignette tính per-trip commission, không phải fee cố định. Asset-heavy mâu thuẫn với "không own vehicles". Cost leadership không match — Uber thường premium-priced surge.
✗ Wrong — C
Advertising = thu tiền advertiser, user free (Google, Meta). Firm thu commission từ driver, không phải ad. Variable-heavy có thể partially correct nhưng "differentiation" không phải điểm chính — đây là platform/network effects play.
Key takeaway
Marketplace = (1) 2 user groups kết nối, (2) take rate trên GMV, (3) asset-light (không own inventory), (4) network effects. Memorize archetype: Uber/Airbnb/Etsy/Visa đều theo pattern này. Dynamic pricing là common feature, không phải defining.

Cost structure & leverage.

§ Vignette
A streaming company spends $15B per year on content production and licensing (largely fixed once committed) and has variable cost per incremental subscriber that is nearly zero. Compared with a peer that uses a variable-heavy cost model, the streaming firm most likely has:
  1. ALower operating leverage and more stable margins through cycles.
  2. BHigher operating leverage — earnings amplify both upward when subscribers grow and downward when they fall.
  3. CIdentical operating leverage because total cost equals fixed plus variable in both cases.

Hint · Question 02

Spot the keywords.

§ Keywords $15B fixed upfront. Variable cost per sub ≈ 0. So sánh với firm variable-heavy. Hỏi operating leverage.

Dịch đề.

§ Vignette
Một streaming company chi $15B/năm cho content production và licensing (phần lớn fixed sau khi commit) và có variable cost cho mỗi subscriber tăng thêm gần như bằng 0. So với firm cùng ngành dùng cost model variable-heavy, streaming firm này nhiều khả năng có:
  1. AOperating leverage thấp hơn và margin ổn định hơn qua cycle.
  2. BOperating leverage cao hơn — earnings khuếch đại cả khi subscriber tăng và khi giảm.
  3. COperating leverage giống nhau vì tổng cost = fixed + variable trong cả hai trường hợp.
CFA Level I Corporate · 05.06 — Business Models
05.06 — Business Models26 / 30
Answer · Question 02

Answer: B — Higher leverage.

✓ Correct — B
Fixed cost cao + variable cost ≈ 0 → mỗi subscriber thêm đóng góp gần 100% vào contribution margin. Khi revenue grow > fixed cost threshold, profit explode (operating leverage cao). Ngược lại, khi subscriber giảm, fixed cost vẫn phải chi → losses amplify. Đây là archetypal high-operating-leverage business: Netflix, Spotify, telecom.
✗ Wrong — A
Đảo ngược logic. Variable-heavy mới có operating leverage thấp + margin ổn định (cost scale với revenue). Fixed-heavy = exact opposite — profit boom-or-bust theo volume.
✗ Wrong — C
"Tổng cost identical" không liên quan operating leverage. Leverage là về mix fixed vs variable, không phải total. 2 firm cùng total cost có thể có leverage rất khác nhau.
Key takeaway
Operating leverage = sensitivity của profit với revenue. Fixed-heavy = high leverage → margin expand mạnh khi grow, contract mạnh khi shrink. Variable-heavy = stable margin nhưng less upside. Common high-leverage industries: airlines, software, streaming, telecom, semiconductor fabs.

Practice · Question 03 · Original

Interpret the pivot.

§ Vignette
A software firm previously sold perpetual licenses at $600 per copy. It transitions to a cloud subscription at $20/month. After the pivot, an analyst observes: GAAP revenue declines in year 1, but ARR grows steadily, churn is low, and the stock's P/E multiple expands. Which interpretation is most consistent with these observations?
  1. AThe pivot is failing — declining revenue signals customers reject subscriptions.
  2. BYear-1 revenue dip reflects timing (recognition over time vs upfront), while market re-rates the firm on visibility/quality of recurring revenue.
  3. CThe multiple expansion is unrelated to the pivot — it is purely macro-driven.

Hint · Question 03

Spot the keywords.

§ Keywords Pivot perpetual $600 upfront → sub $20/mo. Year-1 GAAP revenue declines nhưng ARR tăng, churn low, P/E expand.
  1. Hint 1 Recognition: $600 upfront recorded ngay; $20/mo recorded over time (12 × $20 = $240 in year 1). Vì vậy GAAP revenue dip trong transition là expected, không phải failure.
  2. Hint 2 ARR tăng + churn thấp = leading indicator của future revenue stable. Đây là why analyst nhìn ARR + NDR thay vì chỉ GAAP revenue.
  3. Hint 3 P/E expand = market re-rate firm vì recurring revenue đáng giá hơn lumpy revenue. Adobe pivot 2013 là textbook case.

Dịch đề.

§ Vignette
Một software firm trước đây bán perpetual license giá $600/copy. Firm chuyển sang cloud subscription $20/tháng. Sau pivot, analyst quan sát: GAAP revenue giảm trong năm 1, nhưng ARR tăng đều, churn thấp, P/E multiple expand. Cách diễn giải nào nhất quán nhất với các quan sát này?
  1. APivot đang thất bại — revenue giảm là dấu hiệu customer reject subscription.
  2. BRevenue dip năm 1 phản ánh timing (recognition over time thay vì upfront), trong khi market re-rate firm dựa trên visibility/quality của recurring revenue.
  3. CMultiple expand không liên quan pivot — thuần do macro.
CFA Level I Corporate · 05.06 — Business Models
05.06 — Business Models30 / 30
Answer · Question 03

Answer: B — Re-rating.

✓ Correct — B
Recognition mechanics: $600 upfront → ghi nhận hết ngay. $20/mo → ghi nhận theo tháng → year-1 chỉ ghi $240 dù LTV cao hơn. Revenue dip ngắn hạn là artifact của accounting timing, không phải demand fail. ARR tăng + churn thấp chứng minh underlying business healthier. Market re-rate P/E lên vì recurring revenue được valued multiple cao hơn lumpy revenue (10-20× ARR vs 1-3× revenue). Adobe 2013 stock 10× trong 7 năm là textbook example.
✗ Wrong — A
Misreading. Nếu pivot fail → ARR sẽ giảm hoặc churn sẽ cao, không phải "ARR tăng đều, churn thấp". Revenue dip ngắn hạn không equal failure khi underlying metrics (ARR, retention) tốt.
✗ Wrong — C
"Purely macro" ignore firm-specific evidence. P/E expansion paired với ARR growth + low churn = firm-specific re-rating, không phải sector beta. Macro có thể tài trợ một phần nhưng không "purely".
Key takeaway
Pivot perpetual → subscription thường có year-1 revenue dip do recognition timing — đó là feature, không phải bug. Look at ARR, NDR, churn để judge underlying health. Market re-rate vì subscription = (1) predictable cash flow, (2) higher LTV per customer, (3) stickier moat. Multiple expansion là reward.
